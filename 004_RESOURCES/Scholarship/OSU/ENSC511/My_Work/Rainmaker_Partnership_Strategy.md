## B. FUNGAL SPORE ATMOSPHERIC TRANSPORT: Natural Process Optimized

### Spore Flight Dynamics (PNAS 2020 validated)
**Key finding:** Fungal spores don't disperse randomly—timing of release matters dramatically.

**Flight time statistics:**
- **Day release (high turbulence):** 3-7 day atmospheric flight duration
- **Night release (weak turbulence):** 2-6 hour flight duration
- **Geographic variation:** Southern regions (Mexico/Edwards Plateau latitude) show stronger day/night turbulence cycles

**Implication for bio-balls:**
Deployment at 06:00-09:00 hours local time minimizes atmospheric suspension, ensuring pods settle to soil surface within 2-6 hours. This prevents loss to regional wind transport and maximizes soil contact probability.

### Bioaerosol Budget (Global estimates)
- Estimated global fungal spore emissions: 62 m⁻² s⁻¹ (annual average)
- Ectomycorrhizal fungi (Ascomycota + Basidiomycota) account for ~25% of coarse aerosol particles in temperate forests
- Your inoculated pod deployment (1000-2000 pods/hectare) would deliver **10-100x higher local fungal spore concentrations** than ambient atmosphere

**Operational significance:** High local inoculum concentration (pods as point sources) overwhelms native fungal competition, ensuring ECM fungal establishment priority.

## C. HYDROGEL DISSOLUTION KINETICS: Rainfall Timing Critical

### Hydrogel Water Absorption (Physics well-understood)
- Superabsorbent hydrogels (SAH) achieve 5000-6000% water swelling ratio within hours
- Dissolution rate depends on:
  1. **Rainfall intensity** (higher intensity = faster matrix hydration)
  2. **Soil texture** (sandy soils = faster drainage; clay soils = slower penetration)
  3. **Soil pH** (alkaline soils = slightly slower dissolution)
- Edwards Plateau typical profile: clay/caliche dominance → slower hydrogel dissolution (6-48 hour window) optimal

### Why Cloud-Seeding Enhances This:
Cloud-seeded storms provide:
- **Duration extension:** 22 minutes longer (41% increase) = sustained soil moistening
- **Coverage area:** 44% larger = greater probability hydrogel reaches soil saturation
- **Precipitation certainty:** 70-75% of seeded storms enhance rainfall vs. 40% baseline probability

## D. SOIL MICROBIAL COMMUNITY ASSEMBLY: Active Inoculation Works

### Active vs. Passive Inoculation (New Phytologist 2024)
**Field experiment (urban green roofs, 2-year study):**
- **Passive dispersal only:** 2% of AM fungi came from atmospheric sources, 30% unknown origin
- **Active inoculation:** 45% of AM fungi detected in inoculum established in treatment plots; unique 30% in inoculated vs. uninoculated
- **Result:** Active inoculation created "more distinct and diverse AM fungal communities" that "intensified over time"

**Implication:** Bio-balls as active inoculation source overcome dispersal limitation—the primary constraint on natural microbial community recovery.

### ECM Fungal Colonization Success Rates
- **Nursery conditions (sterile soil):** 80-90% root colonization within 6 weeks
- **Field conditions (natural soil microbiota):** 30-60% colonization (wild fungal competition)
- **Critical distance:** ECM benefits strongest within 15-18 meters of mature oak overstory (existing mycorrhizal networks reduce competition)
