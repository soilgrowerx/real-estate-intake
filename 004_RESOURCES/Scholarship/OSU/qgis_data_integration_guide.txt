This guide provides instructions for connecting QGIS to key data sources for ecological and environmental analysis.

### 1. The USGS National Map: Your Foundation
This is one of the most important sources for US-based data, including elevation, hydrography, and boundaries. We'll connect to it as a Web Map Service (WMS).

**Instructions:**
1.  In QGIS, go to the **Browser Panel** (usually on the left).
2.  Right-click on **WMS/WMTS** and select **New Connection...**.
3.  Fill in the details like this:
    *   **Name:** `USGS National Map`
    *   **URL:** `https://services.nationalmap.gov/arcgis/services/TNM_Vector_Services/MapServer/WMSServer`
4.  Click **OK**.

After connecting, you can expand the `USGS National Map` entry in your Browser Panel to see dozens of layers you can drag and drop into your map.

### 2. Oregon GEOHub (formerly Spatial Data Library)
This is a critical resource for high-resolution, state-specific data for your work at Oregon State.

**Instructions to add a dataset (e.g., Statewide Land Cover):**
1.  Go to the Oregon GEOHub website: https://geohub.oregon.gov/
2.  Use the search bar to find a dataset you need (e.g., `land cover`).
3.  On the dataset's page, find the API or Services link and copy the **WMS** or **ArcGIS REST Service** URL.
4.  Back in QGIS, open the WMS/WMTS connection dialog again and create a new connection (e.g., `Oregon GEOHub - Land Cover`) using the URL you copied.
5.  Repeat this process for other essential datasets like hydrography, administrative boundaries, etc.

### 3. Global Satellite & Aerial Imagery

**A. Quick Basemaps (via QuickMapServices Plugin):**
*   For high-resolution visual context, use the layers provided by the **QuickMapServices** plugin.
*   Access them from the menu: `Web` > `QuickMapServices`. Selections like Google Satellite or Google Hybrid are excellent starting points.

**B. Sentinel-2 Scientific Imagery (via SCP Plugin):**
*   For multispectral analysis, Sentinel-2 data is crucial. The **Semi-Automatic Classification Plugin (SCP)** is the best way to access it.
*   **Instructions:**
    1.  In QGIS, open the SCP dock: `SCP` > `SCP dock`.
    2.  Go to the **Download products** tab.
    3.  Enter your login credentials for the Copernicus Open Access Hub (registration is free on their website).
    4.  Use the map to define your area of interest, search for recent images, and download the required bands directly into your project.

### 4. Key Data Download Portals
For offline use and more intensive analysis, download raw data from these portals.

*   **USGS EarthExplorer (earthexplorer.usgs.gov):**
    *   **Primary Use:** Downloading Landsat satellite imagery, Digital Elevation Models (DEMs), and high-resolution aerial photography.
    *   **Workflow:** Define your area of interest, select the dataset, filter by date or cloud cover, and download the files to your computer.

*   **Natural Earth Data (naturalearthdata.com):**
    *   **Primary Use:** Global, medium-resolution (1:10m, 1:50m, 1:110m scale) cultural and physical vector data.
    *   **Best For:** Creating clean, simple base maps for regional or global analysis where high precision is not required.
