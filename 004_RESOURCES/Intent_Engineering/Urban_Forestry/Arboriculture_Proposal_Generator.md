**To:** [CLIENT_NAME]
**From:** George Steward, Soil Grower (Subcontractor)
**Project:** Soil Health Improvement for [NUMBER_OF_TREES] Trees at [PROJECT_LOCATION]

### Executive Summary
This proposal outlines a targeted plan to improve the soil health and long-term vitality of [NUMBER_OF_TREES] trees at [PROJECT_LOCATION]. The work is divided into two scopes: a full soil invigoration for eight designated trees and targeted soil decompaction for the remaining eight. This approach addresses known soil compaction and other impacts from the recent construction, promoting healthier root growth and overall tree resilience. Soil Grower will manage all aspects of the on-site soil treatment, providing a turnkey solution. The total project cost is **$[TOTAL_COST]**.

### Background and Objectives
New construction sites present significant challenges to mature trees, primarily through soil compaction which limits oxygen and water availability to root systems. This project's objective is to mitigate these stressors by physically decompacting the soil and, where specified, re-introducing vital nutrients and organic matter to create an optimal growing environment for the trees' long-term survival.

### Scope of Work
This project will be executed across two distinct scopes for a total of sixteen (16) trees, covering approximately [TOTAL_SQ_FT] sq ft.

**Scope 1: Full Soil Invigoration (8 Trees - Green Markings)**

1.  **Soil Decompaction:** Utilize a supersonic air tool (Air Spade) to decompact the soil within the critical root zones (CRZ) to a depth of approximately 6-8 inches, fracturing compacted layers and improving soil aeration.
2.  **Amendment Incorporation:** Based on typical needs for post-construction sites, the following amendments will be incorporated into the decompacted soil profile:
    *   High-quality organic compost.
    *   A balanced fertilizer mix, including Nitrogen and Phosphorus, to address nutrient deficiencies.
    *   Biochar and Sulfur to improve soil structure, water retention, and nutrient availability.

**Scope 2: Targeted Air Tilling (8 Trees - Red Markings)**

1.  **Soil Decompaction:** Utilize a supersonic air tool (Air Spade) to till and aerate the soil within the critical root zones (CRZ) to a depth of approximately 6-8 inches to alleviate compaction and enhance oxygen and water penetration.

*Please note: Per your request, this proposal does not include the application or hauling of mulch.*
