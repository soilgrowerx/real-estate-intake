# Elite AI Mega-Prompts

## Writing & Communications

### Mega-Prompt: Persona-Driven Content Engine

> **Use Case:** To generate written content (articles, emails, reports, cover letters, social media posts) that strictly adheres to a specific persona, voice, and set of stylistic rules. This is ideal for maintaining brand consistency or a personal academic voice.

```markdown
# [PROMPT-ROLE: PERSONA-DRIVEN CONTENT ENGINE]

## 1. PERSONA & VOICE DEFINITION
- **Identity:** [e.g., George Steward, a business owner (Soil Grower), graduate student, and military veteran.]
- **Persona Name:** [e.g., "The Neocambrian Architect"]
- **Core Tone & Philosophy:** [e.g., Ferociously Benevolent. A blend of ancient biological wisdom and high-tech systems engineering. Grounded in "dirt-under-fingernails" reality. Rejects "Carbon Tunnel Vision" and believes nature is an asset, not a free input.]

## 2. STRICT WRITING RULES
- **Rule 1:** [e.g., Zero Em Dashes. Strictly forbidden. Use periods or sentence structure to separate thoughts.]
- **Rule 2:** [e.g., Signal-First. No fluff or conversational filler. Every sentence must be structural infrastructure.]
- **Rule 3:** [e.g., Use active voice and avoid passive constructions.]
- **[Add other stylistic constraints as needed]**

## 3. CONTENT TASK
- **Content Type:** [e.g., Cover Letter, Academic Discussion Post, Project Proposal, Email to a client]
- **Objective:** [e.g., To persuade the hiring manager at SkyFi that my operational experience in real estate is directly transferable to a Customer Success role in Earth observation data.]
- **Target Audience:** [e.g., Hiring Team at a fast-paced tech startup; University Professor and peers in an environmental policy course.]
- **Key Information to Include:**
    - [Point 1: e.g., My experience managing multi-million dollar transactions demonstrates my ability to handle complex, high-stakes client relationships.]
    - [Point 2: e.g., My ability to translate technical details for different stakeholders (executives vs. contractors) is a core skill for customer success.]
    - [Point 3: e.g., Acknowledge my personal passion for ecological resilience and how it connects to the company's mission.]
- **Desired Length:** [e.g., Approximately 400 words, single-spaced.]

## 4. EXECUTION
- Generate the [Content Type] based on all the specifications above.
- After drafting, perform a self-correction pass to ensure all **Strict Writing Rules** have been followed without exception.
```

## Research & Analysis

### Mega-Prompt: Exhaustive Deep Research Protocol

> **Use Case:** To initiate a comprehensive research project on a complex topic. This prompt forces the AI to first structure its approach into a clear plan, which the user can approve or amend before the AI executes the full research and synthesis.

```markdown
# [PROMPT-ROLE: DEEP RESEARCH STRATEGIST & SYNTHESIZER]

## 1. RESEARCH DIRECTIVE
- **Primary Subject:** [e.g., Installing a Linux-based LLM development environment on a specific Windows laptop.]
- **Core Objective:** [e.g., Determine the optimal Linux distribution and software stack for building and deploying AI agents for business tasks, with a preference for open-source solutions like Llama.]
- **Key Questions to Answer:**
    - [Question 1: e.g., What are the hardware compatibility issues with the HP ProBook 465 and various Linux distros?]
    - [Question 2: e.g., What is the best open-source alternative to the Nvidia DIGITS platform for agentic work on a non-Nvidia machine?]
    - [Question 3: e.g., What are the practical steps for a dual-boot setup of Windows 11 Pro and the recommended Linux distro?]
    - [Question 4: e.g., What are the regulatory and ethical considerations for deploying AI agents that collect payments for clients?]

## 2. ACTION: STEP 1 - CREATE RESEARCH PLAN
- Your first task is to **propose a detailed, step-by-step research plan** to address my directive.
- The plan should be a numbered list of actions you will take.
- Example Action: "(1) Analyze the hardware specifications of the HP ProBook 465 (Ryzen 7 7735U) to identify known Linux compatibility issues, focusing on kernel support for the integrated GPU and Wi-Fi chipset."
- **Do not execute the research yet.** Wait for my approval of the plan.

## 3. ACTION: STEP 2 - EXECUTE & SYNTHESIZE (Upon Approval)
- Once I approve the plan, you will execute it exhaustively.
- Synthesize all findings into a structured report formatted in Markdown.
- The report must include:
    - **Executive Summary:** A brief, high-level recommendation.
    - **Comparative Analysis:** A table comparing the top [e.g., 3 Linux distributions] based on criteria such as ease of installation, hardware support, and AI/ML library availability.
    - **Step-by-Step Guide:** A detailed walkthrough for the recommended setup.
    - **Risks & Considerations:** A section outlining potential challenges, legal issues, and ethical guidelines.
    - **Source Attribution:** A clear list of all scholarly articles, technical documentation, and forum threads consulted.
```

## Strategic Planning & Business

### Mega-Prompt: Strategic Feasibility & Options Analysis

> **Use Case:** To act as a strategic consultant, analyzing a new business idea, project, or technical proposal. This prompt guides the AI to break down the concept into its core components and evaluate its viability from multiple perspectives.

```markdown
# [PROMPT-ROLE: STRATEGIC FEASIBILITY ANALYST]

## 1. CORE PROPOSAL
- **Concept:** [e.g., Develop a decentralized network of mobile, transportable units for processing yaupon holly into a matcha-style product.]
- **Hypothesis:** [e.g., A mobile processing model will increase supply chain efficiency by processing raw materials at the harvest site, ensuring freshness and reducing logistical costs compared to fixed regional hubs.]

## 2. ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK
- You will analyze this proposal through the following lenses. For each lens, identify key questions, potential advantages, and critical risks.

### A. Technical Feasibility
- **Key Questions:** [e.g., What equipment is required for steaming/drying? What are the power, water, and waste management requirements? Can this be containerized?]
- **Advantages:** [e.g., Standardized equipment, rapid deployment.]
- **Risks:** [e.g., Power supply limitations in remote areas, equipment maintenance challenges.]

### B. Operational & Logistical Feasibility
- **Key Questions:** [e.g., How will units be transported and scheduled? How will quality control be maintained across different locations and operators? What is the user flow for harvesters delivering materials?]
- **Advantages:** [e.g., Reduced spoilage, access to more harvest sites.]
- **Risks:** [e.g., Complex scheduling, potential for quality variance.]

### C. Economic Feasibility (CapEx vs. OpEx)
- **Key Questions:** [e.g., What is the cost of a single mobile unit vs. a regional hub? What are the variable operating costs (fuel, labor, maintenance)?]
- **Analysis:** Create a comparative cost model (high-level) for a mobile network versus a fixed-hub model.

### D. Scalability & Business Model
- **Key Questions:** [e.g., Is a franchise model viable for operators? What are the training and legal requirements? How does this integrate with the STIM.bio community platform?]
- **Advantages:** [e.g., Lower barrier to entry for operators, rapid network expansion.]
- **Risks:** [e.g., Brand dilution, quality control at scale.]

## 3. FINAL DELIVERABLE
- Synthesize the complete analysis into a structured report.
- Conclude with a "Recommendation & Next Steps" section that provides a clear verdict on the proposal's feasibility and outlines the critical next steps for validation (e.g., "Build a prototype unit for field testing and data collection on energy consumption and throughput.").
```
