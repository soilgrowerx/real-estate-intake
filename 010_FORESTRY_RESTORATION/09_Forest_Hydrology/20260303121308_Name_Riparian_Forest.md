Name: Riparian Forest

cottonwood, and red alder. It includes a 70-acre surge plain, a tidal 
freshwater wetland supporting dense shrubs like salmonberry and 
snowberry. This habitat is vital for numerous bird species, including 
waterfowl, raptors, and passerines. (pp 3-21, 3-22)

Habitat 2 Description
Name: Estuarine Saltmarsh (Vegetated Intertidal)
Number of Acres: 633 acres (p 3-13)
Description: Nisqually Salt Marshes are vibrant tidal habitats that are 
regularly ﬂooded, creating essential breeding and feeding grounds for 
migratory birds, waterfowl, and aquatic species. Divided into low, middle,

pickleweed, Lyngby’s sedge, and seaside arrowgrass. The marshes also 
provide vital resources for wildlife, showcasing some of the highest 
vegetation productivity rates in the Paciﬁc Northwest. (p 3-19)
