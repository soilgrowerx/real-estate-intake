Billy Frank Jr. Nisqually National Wildlife Refuge: Protecting
### Habitats and Species
### Final Project
●   By: George Steward
●   Course: EVSP413
●   Date: December 29th, 2024
### Introduction
●   Located in Southern Puget Sound, Washington, in the Nisqually River Delta.
### Location and Signiﬁcance
●   One of the few undeveloped estuaries, crucial for migratory birds along the Paciﬁc Flyway.
●   Regionally important for federally listed fall Chinook salmon.
●   The refuge provides habitat for over 200 bird species and is a critical stopover point for
those migrating along the Paciﬁc Flyway (Byrd et al., 2023).
●   The Nisqually River Delta is a vital ecological area that supports diverse wildlife and plant
species.
●   Objectives include habitat preservation, species protection, and ecosystem restoration.
### Objectives and Current Land
Use                           ●   Managed for conservation, recreation, and environmental education.
●   The refuge aims to balance ecological health with public access and enjoyment.
●   Land use is focused on maintaining natural habitats while providing educational
opportunities.
