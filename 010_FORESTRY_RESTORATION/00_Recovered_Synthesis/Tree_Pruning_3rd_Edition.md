### Pruning live branches reduces a tree’s capacity to photo-
synthesize and manufacture sugar. Pruning also creates
wounds the tree must expend energy to close and defend.
### Therefore, the costs and benefits of pruning healthy
branches should be assessed when developing pruning
objectives. Routine live-branch removal does not improve
tree health, and some practices, such as lion-tailing, can
have adverse effects on tree health and can increase the
likelihood of branch failure.
