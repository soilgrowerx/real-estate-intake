1. Houston Black clay (HoB) - 42,402.3 acres (5.8% of the area)
○ Properties: Found on 1 to 3 percent slopes, this soil is a deep, fertile clay type
that supports robust agricultural activities, particularly crops like cotton and
sorghum.
2. Branyon clay (BrB) - 40,706.1 acres (5.6% of the area)
○ Properties: Found on 1 to 3 percent slopes, this clay soil has high water
retention and nutrient content, ideal for farming but can be challenging due to
slow drainage.
3. Eckrant cobbly clay (EaD) - 53,544.8 acres (7.4% of the area)
○ Properties: Found on 1 to 8 percent slopes, this cobbly clay has limited depth to
bedrock, often used for grazing or wildlife habitat rather than intensive cultivation.
4. Eckrant stony clay (EeB) - 48,225.0 acres (6.6% of the area)
○ Properties: Found on 0 to 3 percent slopes, it has a high stone content, making
it suitable for rangeland and less favorable for crop production.
5. Denton silty clay (DnB) - 30,183.2 acres (4.2% of the area)
○   Properties: Found on 1 to 3 percent slopes, this silty clay has good
nutrient-holding capacity and is often used for agriculture, including grain and
forage crops.
