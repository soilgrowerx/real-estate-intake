### New Veteran-Owned Business
### Pre-Qualification Process
### Business Entity Formation Fee
and Franchise Tax Waiver
### Hi! You may have heard of the new benefits that remove barriers for veterans who want
to form a business. Effective January 2022, the benefit waives the formation fee for new
100% veteran-owned business entities and relieves veteran-owned businesses of the
Texas franchise tax for the initial 5-year period. Don’t get too excited! All businesses are
required to file a franchise tax “No Tax Due” report every year and remember to check
the Veteran-Owned Business box so your business remains in good standing.
### Will your business qualify? If you answer “yes” to these questions, it may:
•   Will this entity be 100% veteran-owned (can be one or more veteran owners)?
•   Is/Are the veteran business owner(s) honorably discharged from active military
duty, from any branch of the United States military, National Guard or Reserves? *
•   Will this entity be formed between the dates of January 1, 2022, through December 31,
2025?
The following pages outline the veteran pre-qualification process.
*This process serves to verify that the individual submitting documents to the Texas
Veterans Commission (TVC) in accordance with Section 171.0005(b) of the Texas Tax
Code is an Honorably Discharged Veteran of the United States military. In
determining veteran status, we refer to:
• Texas Code 434 § 022 (a) “veteran" means a person who has served in the
army, navy, air force, coast guard, or marine corps of the United States; or (2)
the Texas National Guard as defined by § 431.001 (4) "Texas National Guard"
means the Texas Army National Guard and the Texas Air National Guard.
• 38 U.S. Code § 101 - (2) The term “veteran” means a person who served in the
active military, naval, air, or space service, and who was discharged or released
therefrom under conditions other than dishonorable.
• 13 CFR § 125.11 further states “A Reservist or member of the National Guard
called to Federal active duty or disabled from a disease or injury incurred or
aggravated in line of duty or while in training status also qualify as a veteran.”
### If you have lost or are unable to locate proof of service, contact the National Archives:
https://www.archives.gov/veterans/military-service-records.
We are here to help all veteran entrepreneurs regardless of eligibility. For assistance, contact us
at https://www.tvc.texas.gov/entrepreneurs/contact-vep/.
~ TVC Veteran Entrepreneur Program
