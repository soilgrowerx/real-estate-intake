### Invasive Species Management Guidelines
### Management of invasive plants can help to sustain ecosystem
services provided by natural areas and increase the recreational
value of these lands. However, extensive removal of vegetation
can have many undesired effects. An invasive species
management project should balance invasive plant control with
public safety, protection of sensitive or endangered species,
mitigation of wildfire risk and the safe and effective use of
herbicides, while maintaining effective vegetative cover on the
land.
### Invasive plant management projects should be coordinated with
the Watershed Protection Department’s Environmental Resource
### Management division (ERM) to ensure that an environmentally-sound management plan is in
place. Guidelines for developing a plan can be found here. It is encouraged to discuss ideas for
future projects with ERM prior to submitting an application for grant funding. You can contact:
