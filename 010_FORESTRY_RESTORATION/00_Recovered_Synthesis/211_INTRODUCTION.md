### 21.1      INTRODUCTION
### The term conservation has been used for decades to mean more effcient usage and savings of a
resource. In this chapter, the resource is water. Equivalent terms for conservation are sustainability
(see also Part V, Chapter 32), and more recently green engineering and science (see also Part V,
Chapter 33), and reduce/reuse/recycle. Regardless of which term is used, the need to conserve
through more effcient usage, recycling, and reuse has become popular today. While these techniques
are oftentimes the frst choice of populations located in arid areas or far from an ocean or other water
sources, society can often beneft from these techniques as a means of fnding more freshwater.

### The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) has produced a variety of resources for the public
and commercial sectors highlighting the benefts of water conservation, water use effciency, recy-
cling, and reuse for pollution prevention and resource conservation. The EPA’s Preventing Pollution
through Effcient Water Use [1] is directed toward individuals and communities, summarizing pol-
lution prevention, energy conservation, source water protection, and economic and habitat benefts
to effcient use of water. The EPA’s Cleaner Water through Conservation [2] focuses on the effect of
excessive water use (mostly related to outside landscape and agricultural water use) on water quality
and provides residential and industrial/commercial best practices for water conservation and water
use effciency improvements.
