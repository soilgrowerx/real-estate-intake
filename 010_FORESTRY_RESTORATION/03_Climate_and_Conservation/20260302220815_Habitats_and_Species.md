Introduction
Location and Signiﬁcance
●
Located in Southern Puget Sound, Washington, in the Nisqually River Delta.
●
One of the few undeveloped estuaries, crucial for migratory birds along the Paciﬁc Flyway.
●
Regionally important for federally listed fall Chinook salmon.
●
The refuge provides habitat for over 200 bird species and is a critical stopover point for 
those migrating along the Paciﬁc Flyway (Byrd et al., 2023).
●
The Nisqually River Delta is a vital ecological area that supports diverse wildlife and plant 
species.
Objectives and Current Land 
Use
●
Objectives include habitat preservation, species protection, and ecosystem restoration.
●
Managed for conservation, recreation, and environmental education.
●
The refuge aims to balance ecological health with public access and enjoyment.
●
Land use is focused on maintaining natural habitats while providing educational 
opportunities.
https://wa100.dnr.wa.gov/puget-lowland/nisqually-delta

History and 
Cultural 
Signiﬁcance
Establishment and Renaming
●
Established in 1974 as a wildlife refuge.
●
Renamed Billy Frank Jr. Nisqually NWR in 2015 to honor the Nisqually 
tribal leader and activist (Byrd et al., 2023).
●
Recognized for its role in habitat protection and cultural heritage.
Cultural Ties and Key Events
●
Nisqually Tribe's ancestral connection to the delta for sustenance and 
resources (Byrd et al., 2023).
●
Demonstrates a deep understanding of the ecosystem, maintained for 
thousands of years.
●
Key events include the 1904 Brown Farm Dike construction and the 2009 
estuary restoration, the largest in the Paciﬁc Northwest (Byrd et al., 
2023).
