### Question 6: 
An urban economist is curious if the distribution in where Oregon residents live is different today than it was in 1990. She observes that today there are approximately 3,109 thousand residents in NW Oregon, 902 thousand residents in SW Oregon, 244 thousand in Central Oregon, and 102 thousand in Eastern Oregon. She knows that in 1990 the breakdown was as follows: 72.7% NW Oregon, 20.7% SW Oregon, 4.8% Central Oregon, and 2.8% Eastern Oregon.

**Can she conclude that the distribution in residence is different today at a 0.05 level of significance?**

**To calculate the p-value, we will perform a Chi-Square Goodness of Fit test.**

### Steps:

1. **Determine the expected frequencies:**
   - Total population today = 3,109 + 902 + 244 + 102 = 4,357 thousand.
   - Expected frequency for NW Oregon = 72.7% of 4,357 = 0.727 * 4,357 = 3,166.879
   - Expected frequency for SW Oregon = 20.7% of 4,357 = 0.207 * 4,357 = 901.299
   - Expected frequency for Central Oregon = 4.8% of 4,357 = 0.048 * 4,357 = 209.136
   - Expected frequency for Eastern Oregon = 2.8% of 4,357 = 0.028 * 4,357 = 121.996

2. **Calculate the Chi-Square statistic:**
   - \( \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(Observed - Expected)^2}{Expected} \)
   - For NW Oregon: \( \frac{(3,109 - 3,166.879)^2}{3,166.879} = 1.166 \)
   - For SW Oregon: \( \frac{(902 - 901.299)^2}{901.299} = 0.0006 \)
   - For Central Oregon: \( \frac{(244 - 209.136)^2}{209.136} = 5.915 \)
   - For Eastern Oregon: \( \frac{(102 - 121.996)^2}{121.996} = 3.412 \)
   - Total \( \chi^2 \) value = 1.166 + 0.0006 + 5.915 + 3.412 = 10.4936

3. **Degrees of freedom:**
   - \( df = \text{Number of categories} - 1 = 4 - 1 = 3 \)

4. **Determine the p-value using Google Sheets:**
   - Use the formula: `=CHISQ.DIST.RT(10.4936, 3)`

**Google Sheets Formula:**
```excel
=CHISQ.DIST.RT(10.4936, 3)
```

5. **Interpret the result:**
   - If the p-value is less than 0.05, we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the distribution of the population has changed.

**Answer:**
- **p-value = 0.0147**
