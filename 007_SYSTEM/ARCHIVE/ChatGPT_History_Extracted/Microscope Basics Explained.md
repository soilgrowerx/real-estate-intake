3. The coarse adjustment knob is used for making large, rapid changes in focus, especially useful when you are initially bringing your sample into view. **ASSISTANT**:
In the image, the question asks what action to take if you&#x27;re having trouble focusing on a slide with the high-power lens after focusing with a low-power lens:

The correct action to resolve the issue would be to: Return to the low-power lens, refocus the image, and return back to the high-power lens.

Switching back to the low-power lens allows you to re-establish a clear focus on your specimen before carefully switching to the high-power lens again. This is important because the high-power lens has a very short depth of field, and even a slight misalignment can put the specimen out of focus.
