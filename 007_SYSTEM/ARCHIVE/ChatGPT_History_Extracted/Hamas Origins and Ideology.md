Where do Hamas originate?

---

**ASSISTANT**:
Hamas, an acronym for Harakat al-Muqawamah al-‘Islamiyyah which translates to Islamic Resistance Movement, emerged in the late 1980s, specifically during the First Intifada, or uprising, against Israeli occupation in 1987. The roots of Hamas trace back to the broader Muslim Brotherhood movement, which began in Egypt in the 1920s. The Muslim Brotherhood&#x27;s ideological and organizational frameworks significantly influenced the founders of Hamas.

The creation of Hamas was driven by a blend of nationalism and religious identity. It sought to combine the struggle for national liberation with Islamic beliefs, in a bid to liberate Palestine from Israeli occupation and establish an independent Palestinian state inclusive of the West Bank, Gaza Strip, and East Jerusalem.

The emergence of Hamas also was seen as a reaction to the political failures and corruption of secular Palestinian politics, particularly the Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO). By providing social services, education, and healthcare in the Palestinian territories, Hamas built a base of support among the populace, while its militant wing carried out attacks against Israeli military and civilian targets.

Hamas&#x27;s charter, penned in 1988, underlined the group&#x27;s religious and militant approach to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. Over time, Hamas has become a key political and militant actor in the region, especially in the Gaza Strip where it seized power in 2007. However, its designation as a terrorist organization by many countries, including the U.S. and Israel, its armed resistance against Israel, and its governance of the Gaza Strip, amid ongoing conflict and blockades, have kept it at the center of regional and international controversies.
