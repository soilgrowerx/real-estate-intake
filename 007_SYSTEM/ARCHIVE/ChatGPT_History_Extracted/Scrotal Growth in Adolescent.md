**ASSISTANT**:
A lump or swelling in front of the scrotum in a 15-year-old boy can be due to several possible causes:

1. **Inguinal Hernia**: This occurs when a part of the intestine protrudes through a weak spot in the abdominal muscles into the groin area. It can create a noticeable bulge that may increase in size when standing or coughing and might cause discomfort.

2. **Hydrocele**: This is a fluid-filled sac surrounding a testicle, leading to swelling in the scrotum. While hydroceles are usually painless, they can cause a feeling of heaviness or discomfort.

3. **Enlarged Lymph Nodes**: Swollen lymph nodes in the groin can appear as lumps due to infections or other conditions. They might be tender to the touch.

4. **Epidermoid Cyst**: These are small, benign lumps under the skin caused by blocked hair follicles or skin trauma. They are usually painless unless infected.

5. **Spermatocele**: A fluid-filled cyst that develops in the epididymis (a tube behind the testicles). Spermatoceles are generally painless and might feel like a smooth, firm lump.

6. **Varicocele**: This is an enlargement of the veins within the scrotum, similar to varicose veins. It might cause a dull ache or discomfort, especially after standing for a long time.

Given that the lump is causing slight pain and seems fluid-filled, it would be beneficial to consult a healthcare professional for an accurate diagnosis. They can perform a physical examination and possibly imaging tests to determine the exact cause and recommend appropriate treatment if necessary.
