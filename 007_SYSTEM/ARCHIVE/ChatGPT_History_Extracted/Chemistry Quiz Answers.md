### Question 24
Magnesium oxidizes via the reaction:
\[ 2 \text{Mg} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2 \text{MgO} \]

The reaction has a \(\Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -1203 \text{ kJ}\). How much heat (in kJ) is released when you completely react 3.000 moles of \(\text{O}_2\)?

Since the reaction releases 1203 kJ per mole of \(\text{O}_2\), the heat released for 3 moles would be:
\[ 3 \times (-1203 \text{ kJ}) = -3609 \text{ kJ} \]

The marked answer &quot;-3609 kJ&quot; is correct.

### Question 25
Combustion of hydrogen releases 142 J/g of hydrogen reacted. How many kJ of energy are released by the combustion of 16.0 oz of hydrogen? (1 lb = 16 oz; 1 kg = 2.2 lb)

First, convert 16.0 oz to grams:
\[ 16.0 \text{ oz} \times \frac{1 \text{ lb}}{16 \text{ oz}} \times \frac{1000 \text{ g}}{2.2 \text{ lb}} = 454.545 \text{ g} \]

Then calculate the energy released:
\[ 454.545 \text{ g} \times 142 \text{ J/g} = 64545.39 \text{ J} \]
Convert this to kJ:
\[ 64545.39 \text{ J} \times \frac{1 \text{ kJ}}{1000 \text{ J}} = 64.545 \text{ kJ} \]

The marked answer &quot;64.5 kJ&quot; is correct.

Both answers are accurate. If you need further assistance or explanations, feel free to ask!
