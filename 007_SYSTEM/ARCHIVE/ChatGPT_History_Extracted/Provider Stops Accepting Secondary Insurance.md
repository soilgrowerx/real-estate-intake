# Provider Stops Accepting Secondary Insurance

**USER**:
In Texas, can a medical provider stop taking a secondary insurance while still seeing the patient?

---

**ASSISTANT**:
In Texas, as in most places in the United States, a medical provider has the discretion to choose which insurance plans they accept, including secondary insurance. This decision can be based on various factors such as reimbursement rates, administrative complexities, or contractual agreements with insurance companies. 

If a provider decides to stop accepting a certain secondary insurance, they can still see the patient, but the patient might have to pay out-of-pocket for services that would have been covered by that secondary insurance. The patient would then be responsible for seeking reimbursement from their insurance provider directly.

Patients in this situation should be informed by the provider about any changes in insurance acceptance. It&#x27;s also a good practice for patients to verify insurance acceptance with their provider before each appointment to avoid unexpected costs. 

For specific situations, patients should consult directly with their healthcare provider and their insurance company to understand the full implications of such changes on their healthcare costs and coverage.
