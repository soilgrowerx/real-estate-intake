### Chapter 9 Problem Selection (70-105)

**Problem 70:**
Calculate the molar mass of a gas if a sample weighing 0.564 g occupies 125 mL at 750 torr and 24 °C.

### Solution:

1. **Convert Pressure to Atmospheres:**
   - \( P = \frac{750 \, \text{torr}}{760 \, \text{torr/atm}} = 0.987 \, \text{atm} \)

2. **Convert Volume to Liters:**
   - \( V = \frac{125 \, \text{mL}}{1000 \, \text{mL/L}} = 0.125 \, \text{L} \)

3. **Convert Temperature to Kelvin:**
   - \( T = 24 + 273.15 = 297.15 \, \text{K} \)

4. **Use the Ideal Gas Law to Find Moles (n):**
   - \( PV = nRT \)
   - \( n = \frac{PV}{RT} \)
   - \( n = \frac{(0.987 \, \text{atm})(0.125 \, \text{L})}{(0.0821 \, \text{L·atm/K·mol})(297.15 \, \text{K})} \)
   - \( n = 0.00505 \, \text{mol} \)

5. **Calculate the Molar Mass:**
   - Molar Mass = \( \frac{\text{mass}}{\text{moles}} \)
   - Molar Mass = \( \frac{0.564 \, \text{g}}{0.00505 \, \text{mol}} = 111.68 \, \text{g/mol} \)

### Chapter 5 Problem Selection (51-86)

**Problem 60:**
Calculate the heat required to convert 25 g of ice at -10°C to steam at 120°C.

### Solution:

1. **Calculate the heat to warm the ice from -10°C to 0°C:**
   - \( q = m \cdot c \cdot \Delta T \)
   - \( q = 25 \, \text{g} \cdot 2.09 \, \text{J/g°C} \cdot 10 \, \text{°C} \)
   - \( q = 522.5 \, \text{J} \)

2. **Calculate the heat to melt the ice:**
   - \( q = m \cdot \Delta H_f \)
   - \( q = 25 \, \text{g} \cdot 334 \, \text{J/g} \)
   - \( q = 8350 \, \text{J} \)

3. **Calculate the heat to warm the water from 0°C to 100°C:**
   - \( q = m \cdot c \cdot \Delta T \)
   - \( q = 25 \, \text{g} \cdot 4.18 \, \text{J/g°C} \cdot 100 \, \text{°C} \)
   - \( q = 10450 \, \text{J} \)

4. **Calculate the heat to vaporize the water:**
   - \( q = m \cdot \Delta H_v \)
   - \( q = 25 \, \text{g} \cdot 2260 \, \text{J/g} \)
   - \( q = 56500 \, \text{J} \)

5. **Calculate the heat to warm the steam from 100°C to 120°C:**
   - \( q = m \cdot c \cdot \Delta T \)
   - \( q = 25 \, \text{g} \cdot 2.01 \, \text{J/g°C} \cdot 20 \, \text{°C} \)
   - \( q = 1005 \, \text{J} \)

6. **Sum all the heats:**
   - Total \( q = 522.5 \, \text{J} + 8350 \, \text{J} + 10450 \, \text{J} + 56500 \, \text{J} + 1005 \, \text{J} \)
   - Total \( q = 76827.5 \, \text{J} \)

### Problem 9.70 (Chapter 9 Exercises)

**(a) Outline the steps necessary to answer the question: What volume of C\(_2\)H\(_2\) at 1.005 atm and 12.2 °C is formed by the reaction of 15.48 g of CaC\(_2\) with water?**

1. **Write the balanced chemical equation:**
   \[ \text{CaC}_2 + 2\text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{C}_2\text{H}_2 + \text{Ca(OH)}_2 \]

2. **Calculate the moles of CaC\(_2\):**
   - Find the molar mass of CaC\(_2\).
   - Use the given mass to find moles.

3. **Determine the moles of C\(_2\)H\(_2\) produced:**
   - Use stoichiometry based on the balanced equation.

4. **Use the Ideal Gas Law to find the volume of C\(_2\)H\(_2\):**
   - Convert temperature to Kelvin.
   - Use \( PV = nRT \) to solve for volume \( V \).

**(b) Answer the question.**

1. **Calculate the molar mass of CaC\(_2\):**
   - Molar mass of CaC\(_2\) = 40.08 (Ca) + 2 × 12.01 (C) = 64.10 g/mol

2. **Calculate the moles of CaC\(_2\):**
   - Moles of CaC\(_2\) = \(\frac{15.48 \, \text{g}}{64.10 \, \text{g/mol}} = 0.2415 \, \text{mol}\)

3. **Determine the moles of C\(_2\)H\(_2\) produced:**
   - According to the balanced equation, 1 mole of CaC\(_2\) produces 1 mole of C\(_2\)H\(_2\).
   - Moles of C\(_2\)H\(_2\) = 0.2415 mol

4. **Use the Ideal Gas Law to find the volume of C\(_2\)H\(_2\):**
   - Convert temperature to Kelvin: \( T = 12.2 + 273.15 = 285.35 \, \text{K} \)
   - Use \( PV = nRT \):
     \[
     V = \frac{nRT}{P}
     \]
     Where:
     - \( P = 1.005 \, \text{atm} \)
     - \( n = 0.2415 \, \text{mol} \)
     - \( R = 0.0821 \, \text{L·atm/K·mol} \)
     - \( T = 285.35 \, \text{K} \)

     \[
     V = \frac{(0.2415 \, \text{mol}) (0.0821 \, \text{L·atm/K·mol}) (285.35 \, \text{K})}{1.005 \, \text{atm}} = 5.64 \, \text{L}
     \]

**Answer: The volume of C\(_2\)H\(_2\) formed is 5.64 L.**
