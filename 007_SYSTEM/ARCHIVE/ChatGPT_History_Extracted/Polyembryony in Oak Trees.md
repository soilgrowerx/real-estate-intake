# Polyembryony in Oak Trees

**USER**:
What is it called when an oak tree sprouts two trees from one acorn?

---

**ASSISTANT**:
When an oak tree sprouts two trees from a single acorn, it&#x27;s a phenomenon known as &quot;polyembryony.&quot; This occurs when multiple embryos develop from a single fertilized egg, leading to the emergence of two genetically identical trees, essentially twins, from one acorn. It&#x27;s a rare and fascinating occurrence, George, not just in oaks but in the plant kingdom at large. This phenomenon blurs the lines between the singular identity we often associate with an individual organism and the complex, interconnected nature of life, echoing the kind of holistic perspective you appreciate. Polyembryony challenges our conventional views on individuality in nature and opens up intriguing avenues for understanding biodiversity and ecological relationships.
