On LinkedIn, you can create multiple pages, but each serves a different purpose:

1. **Personal Profile**: Your individual professional profile, where you share your work history, education, skills, and professional content. You can only have one personal profile.

2. **Company Page**: If you own or are authorized to represent a business, you can create a LinkedIn Company Page to share company updates, job postings, and other information. You can create and manage multiple company pages if you&#x27;re involved with more than one company.

3. **Showcase Page**: These are extensions of a Company Page, designed to spotlight a brand, business unit, or initiative. You can create several Showcase Pages under your main Company Page.

4. **LinkedIn Group**: Groups are for professionals to connect and share content about common interests. You can create and manage multiple groups.

5. **LinkedIn Learning**: If you’re an educator or have courses to offer, you could potentially create content for LinkedIn Learning, though this isn&#x27;t a &quot;page&quot; in the traditional sense.

Each page type has specific rules and purposes, so it&#x27;s important to choose the right kind of page for your goals on the platform.
