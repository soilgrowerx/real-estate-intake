**ASSISTANT**:
The chemical formula for the silica tetrahedra, which is the building block of all silicate minerals, is SiO2, not SiO4. The confusion might arise because the silica tetrahedron is a structural unit composed of one silicon atom covalently bonded to four oxygen atoms, but when considering the chemical formula, it is necessary to account for the way these tetrahedra share oxygen atoms with each other in a silicate mineral&#x27;s structure. This sharing reduces the overall number of oxygen atoms required per silicon atom, leading to the chemical formula SiO2, rather than SiO4. This sharing of oxygen atoms is a key characteristic of silicate minerals, leading to various structures such as chains, sheets, and three-dimensional frameworks, depending on how the tetrahedra are arranged and linked.
