# Superintendent Influence on Hiring

**USER**:
how much leverage does a school superintendent have in hiring a network administrator if the director of technology is interviewing people

---

**ASSISTANT**:
A school superintendent typically has significant leverage in hiring decisions, including for a Network Administrator, but their involvement may vary depending on the district’s size, policies, and the relationship between the superintendent and the Director of Technology.

Here’s a breakdown of the typical dynamics:

1. **Final Authority**: The superintendent usually has the final say on hiring decisions for key positions, particularly if the role directly impacts district operations. Even if the Director of Technology conducts the interviews and provides a recommendation, the superintendent might approve or veto the decision.

2. **Delegated Responsibility**: In many cases, the superintendent delegates hiring responsibility to the Director of Technology for positions like Network Administrator. In such cases, the superintendent may still be involved in final approval but will heavily rely on the director&#x27;s judgment.

3. **Strategic Alignment**: The superintendent may ensure that key hires align with broader district goals. If the superintendent has a particular vision for technology in the district, they may influence the decision more directly to ensure the new hire aligns with that vision.

4. **Collaboration with Director of Technology**: If the superintendent and the Director of Technology have a collaborative relationship, the superintendent may seek regular updates or have input at key decision points, even if the Director handles the majority of the hiring process.

In your case, since the superintendent has already referred you to the Director of Technology, it indicates a level of involvement, suggesting that they may have some influence over the final decision. However, the Director of Technology will likely have a significant voice in the decision based on technical expertise and departmental needs.
