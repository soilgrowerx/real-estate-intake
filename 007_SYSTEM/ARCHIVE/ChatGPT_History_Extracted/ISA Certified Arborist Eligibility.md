To qualify for the ISA Certified Arborist® exam, the International Society of Arboriculture (ISA) requires a combination of education and practical experience. Here are the possible combinations of education and experience that can make a candidate eligible for the exam:

1. **Three years of full-time, practical work experience in arboriculture**  
   - This is the standard qualification path for those without formal education in arboriculture or related fields.

2. **Two-year degree in arboriculture and two years of practical work experience**  
   - Degrees like an associate&#x27;s degree from a community college or vocational program focusing on arboriculture, forestry, landscape architecture, or horticulture.

3. **Four-year degree in a related field and one year of practical work experience**  
   - Fields include arboriculture, forestry, horticulture, landscape architecture, or environmental sciences.

4. **Vocational or training programs plus additional practical experience**  
   - Completion of formal apprenticeship programs, technical training programs, or vocational education in arboriculture or a closely related field, followed by additional practical experience, can sometimes be considered. The specifics of this option may vary and require direct confirmation with ISA.

In all cases, practical work experience means hands-on, field-based experience in tree care practices like pruning, planting, diagnosis, and management of tree health. Documentation of work experience (including supervisor or client references) will be required during the application process.

For the ISA Certified Arborist® exam, the verification process typically involves providing details about your work experience, including employer contact information, job duties, and dates of employment. Here&#x27;s what you generally need for each employer verification:

1. **Signed Work Verification Form**: This form is completed by your employer or supervisor, confirming your job role, responsibilities, and duration of employment in arboriculture-related work. ISA may contact the verifier to confirm the information.

2. **Employer Contact Details**: You’ll need to provide current contact information for employers or supervisors, which allows ISA to reach out if further verification is required.

3. **Detailed Job Descriptions**: It helps to include a clear description of the specific arboriculture tasks you performed. This can strengthen the application, especially if you have a diverse range of experiences.

Regarding your situation with part-time work from ages 12 to 18, the challenge is the lack of current contact with the family member who supervised your work. While ISA emphasizes verifiable work experience, they may consider alternative documentation in cases where direct verification is challenging. Some options might include:

- **Affidavits**: You can provide a written statement or affidavit detailing your experience, signed by another person who may have observed your work or can vouch for your experience, even if they weren’t directly your employer.

- **Records or Documentation**: If you have any old records, photos, or logs from your work during that period, these might help support your claim, even if not officially from an employer.

- **References from Related Professionals**: If you later worked for other arboriculture-related employers or professionals who can attest to your skill set and history, they might be able to speak to your background and growth in the field.

Since this scenario is a bit unique, it’s best to contact ISA directly to explain your situation and ask about their willingness to accept alternative verification methods for earlier experiences. They may provide more specific guidance on how to proceed.
