The dinosaur in the picture is a **Stegosaurus**, a well-known herbivorous dinosaur that lived during the Late Jurassic period, around 150 million years ago. 

Here are a few interesting facts about the Stegosaurus:

1. **Distinctive Features**: Stegosaurus is famous for the two rows of large, bony plates along its back and the spikes on its tail, often referred to as the &quot;thagomizer.&quot; These features were likely used for defense and perhaps display.

2. **Size and Structure**: Stegosaurus was a large dinosaur, growing up to 30 feet long, but it had a relatively small head and brain compared to its body size. Despite its small brain, the Stegosaurus was a massive and powerful creature.

3. **Diet**: As a herbivore, Stegosaurus primarily ate low-lying plants such as ferns, cycads, and conifers. It had a beak-like mouth for cropping vegetation, which it probably swallowed whole due to its simple teeth and lack of chewing capability.

4. **Habitat**: The Stegosaurus lived in what is now North America and parts of Europe. It roamed forests and plains, sharing its environment with other famous dinosaurs like Allosaurus and Apatosaurus.
