Here are some study questions for Chapter 1 (Tree Biology) and Chapter 2 (Tree Identification) from the ISA Arborist Certification Study Guide:

### Chapter 1: Tree Biology
1. What are meristems, and where are they located in a tree?
2. Describe the process of photosynthesis and its importance to trees.
3. Explain the function of stomata and their role in tree physiology.
4. What is the relationship between the xylem and phloem in the transport of water, nutrients, and carbohydrates?
5. Define CODIT (Compartmentalization of Decay in Trees) and describe how it helps trees manage damage and decay.
6. What are mycorrhizae, and how do they benefit trees?
7. Describe the difference between excurrent and decurrent tree growth forms.
8. How do trees respond to environmental stimuli such as light and gravity?

### Chapter 2: Tree Identification
1. What is the significance of botanical names in tree identification?
2. List and explain five characteristics commonly used to identify trees.
3. What are the differences between angiosperms and gymnosperms?
4. Explain the differences between simple and compound leaves, and provide examples of trees with each leaf type.
5. What is the importance of leaf arrangement in identifying trees?
6. Describe the morphological differences between palmately compound and pinnately compound leaves.
7. How can bark texture and bud arrangement be used in tree identification?
8. What is the correct way to write a botanical name, and why is it important?

These questions focus on reinforcing key concepts from each chapter and will help guide your study.
