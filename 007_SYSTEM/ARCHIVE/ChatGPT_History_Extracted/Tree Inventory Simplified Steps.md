Here&#x27;s a simplified breakdown of the scope of work for Tree Inventory and Assessment:

### Step 1: Tree Location and Tagging
- Locate all trees within the designated 65-acre area (excluding certain areas with specific size limitations).
- Focus on trees 8 inches or larger in diameter.
- Tag each tree with a medallion and assign a GPS location with 60 cm accuracy.

### Step 2: Data Collection
- Measure each tree’s diameter at breast height (DBH) at 54 inches above ground level, per City of Austin standards.
- Record the species, GPS coordinates (with 60 cm accuracy), and assess the tree’s condition.

### Step 3: Reporting
- Create a summary report that includes:
  - A detailed tree list in Excel with photos of each tree.
  - Links to individual tree reports.
  - A .csv file for integration into design/GIS software.
  - A visual map showing the trees within the property boundaries.
  - Recommendations and specifications for tree care.

### Step 4: GIS Coordination
- Use the NAD 1983 State Plane Texas Central FIPS 4203 US Feet (ESRI:102739) coordinate system for all location data.
- Ensure critical tree locations are verified by a Registered Professional Land Surveyor.

### Optional Step (Expanded Scope)
- Expand to include trees 4 inches or larger in diameter if needed.

This sequence simplifies the process into manageable steps from fieldwork to final reporting and verification.
