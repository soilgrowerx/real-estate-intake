Define mutualism for me.

---

**ASSISTANT**:
Mutualism is a type of ecological interaction or symbiotic relationship between two different species in which both species benefit from the association. In a mutualistic relationship, each organism provides some form of advantage to the other, which may include resources such as food, shelter, or protection.

For example, a classic mutualistic relationship is the one between bees and flowers. Bees benefit by obtaining nectar and pollen from the flowers, which they use as food, while in the process, they also aid in pollinating the flowers, facilitating their reproduction. The flowers, in turn, benefit from the pollination, as it allows them to produce seeds and reproduce.

Mutualism is one of several types of ecological interactions, including commensalism (one species benefits while the other is neither helped nor harmed) and parasitism (one species benefits at the expense of the other).

Here&#x27;s how ecological succession relates to a Miyawaki forest implementation:

1. **Starting Point**: When a Miyawaki forest is first established, it often begins as a collection of pioneer plant species. In summary, ecological succession relates to a Miyawaki forest implementation by highlighting the dynamic and evolving nature of these reforestation projects.
