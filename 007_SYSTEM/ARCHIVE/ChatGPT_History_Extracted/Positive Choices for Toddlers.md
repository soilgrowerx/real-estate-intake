Dealing with a determined and intelligent toddler like Evyn can be challenging, especially during such formative years. Here are some strategies that might help:

### 1. **Positive Reinforcement**
   - **Praise and Rewards:** Reinforce good behavior with praise or small rewards. For instance, when she uses the potty or listens without screaming, celebrate her success with positive words or a sticker chart.
   - **Focus on the Positive:** Acknowledge and reward her efforts to share with Bodie or any other positive actions. This will help her associate good behavior with positive outcomes.

### 2. **Structured Choices**
   - **Empower Her with Choices:** Give Evyn options to choose from within acceptable boundaries. For example, let her decide between two outfits or two snacks. This gives her a sense of control while keeping her choices within limits you set.
   - **Reverse Psychology:** As you mentioned, using reverse psychology can be effective. For example, instead of insisting she use the potty, you might say, &quot;You can use the potty when you&#x27;re ready. It’s your choice.&quot;

### 3. **Consistent Routines**
   - **Predictable Schedule:** Toddlers thrive on routine. Establishing consistent mealtimes, playtimes, and bedtimes can provide a sense of security and reduce tantrums.
   - **Calm Transitions:** When transitioning between activities, give her a heads-up. For example, “In five minutes, we will start getting ready for bed.”

### 4. **Effective Time-Outs**
   - **Short and Sweet:** Time-outs should be brief (1-2 minutes per year of age). Ensure they are consistent and immediate following the undesirable behavior.
   - **Explain and Reflect:** After the time-out, calmly explain why she was given a time-out and discuss better behavior choices.

### 5. **Modeling Calm Behavior**
   - **Stay Calm:** When she screams or says &quot;no,&quot; respond calmly. Children often mirror adult reactions, so staying calm can help her learn to do the same.
   - **Deep Breaths Together:** Continue practicing deep breathing exercises together. Make it a fun and engaging activity by doing it with her and turning it into a game.

### 6. **Handling Potty Training**
   - **Low Pressure:** Avoid pressuring her about using the potty. Make it a no-stress situation by having her favorite books or toys near the potty.
   - **Celebrate Small Wins:** Even if she sits on the potty without using it, celebrate her for trying. Use her favorite songs or dances as a reward for her effort.

### 7. **Sibling Adjustment**
   - **Individual Attention:** Ensure Evyn gets one-on-one time with you, separate from Bodie. This can help her feel special and reduce jealousy.
   - **Encourage Kindness:** Praise her whenever she is kind or helpful to Bodie. Reinforce the idea that she is a great big sister.

### 8. **Professional Guidance**
   - **Consult a Pediatrician or Child Psychologist:** If her behavior continues to be challenging, seeking professional advice can provide personalized strategies and support.

Remember, patience and consistency are key. Each child is unique, and what works for one may not work for another. By giving Evyn a sense of control and reinforcing positive behavior, you&#x27;ll likely see gradual improvements over time.
