# Hyperbola Equation Summary

**ASSISTANT**:
The equation \( \frac{x^2}{4} - \frac{y^2}{25} = 1 \) describes a hyperbola centered at the origin. In this form, the positive term is associated with \( x \), which means the hyperbola opens left and right along the x-axis. Thus, the transverse axis, which is the axis along which the hyperbola opens, is the x-axis.

---

**ASSISTANT**:
The center of the hyperbola described by the equation \( \frac{x^2}{4} - \frac{y^2}{25} = 1 \) is at the point (0, 0). This is because the equation is in standard form with \( x \) and \( y \) terms on the left-hand side being squared and equal to 1 on the right-hand side, indicating that the center is at the origin.

---

**ASSISTANT**:
The vertices of the hyperbola are located at a distance equal to the square root of the term associated with the positive \( x^2 \) term in the equation \( \frac{x^2}{4} - \frac{y^2}{25} = 1 \) from the center along the transverse axis. Here, since the transverse axis is the x-axis, and \( \sqrt{4} = 2 \), the vertices are (2, 0) and (-2, 0).

---

**ASSISTANT**:
The foci of a hyperbola can be found using the equation \( c^2 = a^2 + b^2 \), where \( c \) is the distance from the center to a focus, \( a \) is the distance from the center to a vertex on the transverse axis, and \( b \) is the distance from the center to a vertex on the conjugate axis.

For the hyperbola \( \frac{x^2}{29} - \frac{y^2}{20} = 1 \), we have \( a^2 = 29 \) and \( b^2 = 20 \), thus:

\( c^2 = 29 + 20 = 49 \)
\( c = \sqrt{49} = 7 \)

Since the x-term is positive and appears first, the hyperbola opens left and right, and the foci are located along the x-axis. Therefore, the coordinates of the foci are at (7, 0) and (-7, 0).

---

**ASSISTANT**:
The equation of the hyperbola is given in the form \((y-k)^2/a^2 - (x-h)^2/b^2 = 1\). The center of the hyperbola is at the point \((h, k)\).

For the given hyperbola \((y-3)^2/49 - (x+2)^2/16 = 1\), the center is at the point where \(x = -2\) and \(y = 3\), so the center is \((-2, 3)\).

---

**ASSISTANT**:
The vertices of a hyperbola are given by the points on the transverse axis that are a distance &#x27;a&#x27; from the center of the hyperbola. For the hyperbola defined by the equation \((x-3)^2/49 - (y+2)^2/16 = 1\), it opens along the x-axis since the x-term comes first and is positive.

Here, \(a^2 = 49\), so \(a = \sqrt{49} = 7\). The center of the hyperbola is at \((3, -2)\) as determined from the equation. Thus, the vertices are a distance of &#x27;a&#x27; units from the center along the x-axis, which means the vertices are at \((3+7, -2)\) and \((3-7, -2)\), or \((10, -2)\) and \((-4, -2)\).

**ASSISTANT**:
The equation \((y+4)^2/81 + x^2/16 = 1\) defines an ellipse. This is recognized by the presence of two squared terms on the left side of the equation, both being added together and set equal to 1.

---

**ASSISTANT**:
The equation \((y+4)^2/81 - x^2/16 = 1\) defines a hyperbola. This is indicated by the subtraction between the two squared terms on the left side of the equation, set equal to 1.
