# Mitosis vs. Meiosis: Diploid?

**ASSISTANT**:
The incorrect statement is:

**&quot;Daughter cells are diploid after one round of mitosis, and diploid after one round of meiosis.&quot;**

Explanation: After mitosis, the daughter cells are indeed diploid, meaning they have the same number of chromosomes as the parent cell. However, after meiosis, the daughter cells are haploid, meaning they have half the number of chromosomes as the parent cell. This reduction is crucial for sexual reproduction to maintain the correct chromosome number in offspring.

**Increased species diversity**

Explanation: Crossing over is a process that occurs during meiosis, where homologous chromosomes exchange segments of DNA. This genetic recombination increases genetic variation in a population, which can lead to greater species diversity over time. The new combinations of alleles that result from crossing over can produce individuals with unique genetic makeups, enhancing the genetic diversity of a species.

**ASSISTANT**:
The correct answer is:

**Meiosis I separates homologous chromosomes while Meiosis II separates sister chromatids.**

Explanation: In Meiosis I, homologous chromosomes (each consisting of two sister chromatids) are separated into different cells, reducing the chromosome number by half. This is known as the reductional division. In Meiosis II, the sister chromatids of each chromosome are separated, similar to what happens in mitosis, resulting in four haploid cells. This is called the equational division.

The correct answer is:

**Meiosis results in sex cells that contain one set of genes**

Explanation: Meiosis results in the formation of sex cells, or gametes (sperm and egg cells), which are haploid. This means they contain one set of chromosomes (one set of genes), half the number of chromosomes present in somatic (body) cells, which are diploid and contain two sets of chromosomes. This reduction is essential for sexual reproduction to ensure that when two gametes fuse during fertilization, the resulting zygote has the correct diploid number of chromosomes.
