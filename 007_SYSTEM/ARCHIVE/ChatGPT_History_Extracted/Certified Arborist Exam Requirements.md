# Certified Arborist Exam Requirements

**USER**:
What are the different combinations of prerequisites in order to take the ISA certified arborist exam?

---

**ASSISTANT**:
To become an ISA Certified Arborist, candidates must meet certain prerequisites that combine education and professional experience in arboriculture. The International Society of Arboriculture (ISA) outlines these prerequisites to ensure that candidates have a well-rounded background in tree care practices before they can take the certification exam. Here are the combinations of prerequisites:

1. **Three Years of Full-Time Work Experience in Arboriculture**: This is the most common route, suitable for those who have been working in the field of arboriculture. Experience can include practical, hands-on work in areas such as pruning, tree removal, tree planting, and care, or other related fields.

2. **Two-Year Degree in Arboriculture and Two Years of Full-Time Work Experience**: If you have an associate degree or equivalent in arboriculture or a related field from an accredited institution, you can combine this with two years of full-time work experience in arboriculture to qualify.

3. **Four-Year Degree in a Related Field and One Year of Full-Time Work Experience**: A bachelor’s degree in fields such as horticulture, landscape architecture, forestry, or a related field from an accredited institution, combined with one year of full-time work experience in arboriculture, can also meet the prerequisites.

For all these paths, the experience must be directly related to arboriculture and can include areas such as pruning, fertilization, installation and establishment of trees, diagnosis and treatment of tree problems, cabling and bracing, and/or other tree care services.

It&#x27;s important to note that the ISA periodically reviews and updates its certification requirements. Candidates are encouraged to verify the current prerequisites on the ISA&#x27;s official website or by contacting the ISA directly to ensure they have the most up-to-date information before applying for the exam.
