You&#x27;re probably thinking of &quot;Moana&quot; as part of the phrase &quot;Moana Nui,&quot; which is often used in the context of Hawaiian culture and language to refer to the vast Pacific Ocean. However, the specific word you&#x27;re referring to might be **&quot;Moana Loa&quot;** or **&quot;Moana Nui a Kiwa.&quot;**

- **Moana Loa**: &quot;Moana&quot; means &quot;ocean&quot; in Hawaiian, and &quot;loa&quot; means &quot;long&quot; or &quot;vast.&quot; So, &quot;Moana Loa&quot; can be interpreted as &quot;the great ocean,&quot; symbolizing the expansive nature of the Pacific.

- **Moana Nui a Kiwa**: This term translates to &quot;Great Ocean of Kiwa&quot; and is often used across Polynesia to refer to the Pacific Ocean. Kiwa is a figure in Polynesian mythology associated with the ocean.
