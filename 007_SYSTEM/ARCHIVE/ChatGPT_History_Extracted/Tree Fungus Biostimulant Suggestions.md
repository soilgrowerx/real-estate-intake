### Initial Assessment
1. **Possible Causes**:
   - **Sunscald or Frost Damage**: These can cause dark, damaged areas on tree bark, particularly when trees are young or thin-barked.
   - **Sooty Mold or Fungal Growth**: This can appear if the tree has a sap-sucking insect infestation like aphids, which leave behind honeydew that molds easily.
   - **Mechanical Injury or Chemical Burns**: Damage from physical impact or exposure to harsh chemicals can also create dark spots.
   - **Black Spot Fungus**: Certain fungal pathogens cause dark spots but usually affect leaves more than bark.

2. **Concerns**:

2. **Immediate Steps**:
