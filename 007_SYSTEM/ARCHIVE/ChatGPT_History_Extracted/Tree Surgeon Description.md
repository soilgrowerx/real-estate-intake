**ASSISTANT**:
A tree surgeon, also known as an arborist, is a professional skilled in the art and science of planting, caring for, and maintaining individual trees. Their expertise encompasses a broad range of activities essential for tree health and safety. This includes:

1. **Diagnosis and Treatment**: Identifying diseases, pests, and other issues affecting tree health, and administering appropriate treatments or preventative measures.

2. **Tree Surgery**: Performing complex procedures like pruning, which involves removing dead or overgrown branches to promote tree health and safety. 

3. **Tree Removal**: Safely removing trees, particularly in urban or crowded environments, where their fall could pose risks to people or property.

4. **Maintenance and Care**: Providing ongoing care like fertilization, mulching, and irrigation to enhance tree vitality.

5. **Risk Assessment**: Evaluating trees for potential hazards, such as structural weaknesses, which might necessitate intervention to protect the public.

6. **Planting and Transplanting**: Advising on and executing the planting of new trees, and, when necessary, relocating existing trees.

7. **Consultation and Management**: Offering advice on tree management in various settings, from residential properties to large-scale urban forestry projects.

Tree surgeons must have a deep understanding of tree biology, physiology, and pathology, along with practical skills in climbing and using specialized equipment. Many are certified through organizations like the International Society of Arboriculture (ISA), which requires ongoing education and adherence to a code of ethics, ensuring high standards in tree care and safety practices.
