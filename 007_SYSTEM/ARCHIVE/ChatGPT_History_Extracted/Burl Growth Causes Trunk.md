The bulging of this tree&#x27;s trunk is most likely due to a growth pattern called burl. A burl is a tree growth in which the grain has grown in a deformed manner. It is commonly found in the form of a rounded outgrowth on a tree trunk or branch that is filled with small knots from dormant buds. Burls result from a tree undergoing some form of stress, which could include injury, virus, fungus, infestation, or environmental conditions. The tree&#x27;s response to this stress is the overproduction of growth hormone, causing a proliferation of cell growth at the site of the stress, and this leads to the characteristic bulging growth pattern. Burls are valued for their beauty and uniqueness, especially in woodworking, but they can also indicate that the tree has experienced some form of environmental stress or damage.
