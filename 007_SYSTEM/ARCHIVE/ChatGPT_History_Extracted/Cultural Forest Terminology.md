Forest News
Did you know that pocket forests can prevent floods?
The dense mix of leaves, branches, and trunks found in SUGI Pocket Forests can catch much of the rain, easing its effect. The flow of the water gets trapped in the canopy and a significant percentage evaporates without touching the ground. Deforested areas or surfaces like concrete can’t absorb water. A pocket forest’s permeable soil, on the other hand, soaks up water like a sponge. The root systems also help manage stormwater, absorbing 150% more water than unplanted soil.
