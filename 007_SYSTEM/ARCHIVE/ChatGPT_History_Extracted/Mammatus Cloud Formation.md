The cloud formation in the image appears to be mammatus clouds. These clouds are characterized by their unique, pouch-like structures that hang beneath the base of a larger cloud, often a cumulonimbus. They are usually associated with severe thunderstorms and can be an indicator of turbulent weather conditions.

Mammatus clouds form as a result of sinking air, which contrasts with the rising air typically associated with cloud formation. This sinking air is cooler than the surrounding atmosphere and contains high concentrations of water or ice, which leads to the distinct, bulging shapes.

While they may look ominous, mammatus clouds themselves are not dangerous, though they often signal the presence of severe weather nearby.
