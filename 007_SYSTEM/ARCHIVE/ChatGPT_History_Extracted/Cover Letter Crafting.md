Grey sky requirements refer to the conditions in which a disaster program manager must be able to perform their assigned responsibilities, even if a disaster has not yet occurred but is anticipated or imminent. This means that the manager must be prepared to act and respond to a potential disaster situation, which may include planning, organizing, and coordinating resources and personnel, as well as communicating with stakeholders and partners. In other words, grey sky requirements are a way to ensure that the disaster program manager is always ready to respond to a crisis, whether or not it has actually occurred.
