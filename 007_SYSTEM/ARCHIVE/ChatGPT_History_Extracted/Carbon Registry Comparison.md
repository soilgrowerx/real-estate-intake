**1. Verra (VCS) - ACM003 &amp; VM0047**  
- **Focus:** Verra’s Verified Carbon Standard (VCS) is one of the most widely recognized and utilized carbon registries.  
- **Methodologies:**  
  - **ACM003:** Often deals with afforestation/reforestation activities, focusing on carbon quantification. It’s relatively technical and compliance-oriented.  
  - **VM0047:** Another VCS methodology that might address improved forest management or specific forest restoration approaches.  
- **Advantages:** High market recognition, rigorous standards, and strong credibility in carbon accounting.  
- **Considerations:** While robust, Verra tends to emphasize precise carbon measurements. Co-benefits like biodiversity and local community integration are encouraged but may not be as prominently featured as in some other standards.

**2. ACR Standard (v8.0)**  
- **Focus:** The American Carbon Registry (ACR) sets methodologies for both compliance and voluntary markets in the U.S. and internationally.  
- **Advantages:** Known for scientific rigor, transparency, and strong technical protocols.  
- **Considerations:** Similar to Verra, ACR is technically strong but may place slightly less emphasis on broader social/ecological co-benefits. It’s very credible among carbon offset buyers.

**3. Gold Standard A/R Methodology (v2)**  
- **Focus:** The Gold Standard was established by WWF and other NGOs, known for premium-quality credits that strongly emphasize sustainable development and social/ecological co-benefits.  
- **For A/R (Afforestation/Reforestation):** This methodology ensures projects deliver not just carbon sequestration, but also positive impacts on local communities, biodiversity, and overall ecosystem health.  
- **Advantages:** Credible, community- and biodiversity-focused, and recognized for stringent ethical standards.

**4. Plan Vivo PV Climate (V5.0)**  
- **Focus:** Community-based approaches, ecosystem restoration, and livelihood improvements are central to Plan Vivo.  
- **Advantages:** Strong emphasis on involving local stakeholders, restoring native ecosystems, and enhancing biodiversity, all while delivering carbon benefits.  
- **Considerations:** Often more flexible in tailoring methodologies to local ecological and social contexts. Credibility is growing, especially for projects that prioritize co-benefits over mere carbon accounting.

**5. Ecosystem Restoration Standard (M001)**  
- **Focus:** While details can vary, this type of standard is generally aimed at full-scale ecosystem restoration rather than just carbon.  
- **Advantages:** Emphasizes biodiversity, ecosystem resilience, soil health, and functional ecological restoration. It’s likely more holistic in scope.  
- **Considerations:** Might be newer or less widely recognized than Verra or Gold Standard, but it can align strongly with projects that prioritize ecological integrity.

**6. Climate Action Reserve – Forestry Protocol**  
- **Focus:** North America-based (especially U.S.) with rigorous quantification methods for forestry projects.  
- **Advantages:** Well-recognized, often used in California’s compliance market, ensures transparent carbon accounting.  
- **Considerations:** Primarily focuses on carbon metrics. While biodiversity and sustainable management are factors, co-benefits may not be as central as in Gold Standard or Plan Vivo.
