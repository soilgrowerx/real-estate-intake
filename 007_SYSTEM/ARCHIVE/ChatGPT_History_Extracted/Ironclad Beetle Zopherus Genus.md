- **Safety**: DEET can cause skin irritation, especially at higher concentrations. It has a distinctive odor and can cause eye irritation if not used properly.

### Common Causes of Leaf Spotting on Live Oaks:

1. **Fungal Infections**:
   - **Anthracnose**: This is a common fungal disease that affects Live Oaks. It causes irregular brown or tan spots on the leaves. The spots often have a darker margin and may coalesce to form larger blotches.
   - **Leaf Spot Fungus (Cercospora)**: Causes small, dark spots that can merge into larger areas of dead tissue. The spots may be brown, black, or purple.

2. **Bacterial Infections**:
   - **Bacterial Leaf Scorch (Xylella fastidiosa)**: Symptoms include marginal leaf burn, yellowing, and browning of leaves, often with a yellow halo around the affected areas. This is more severe and can lead to significant damage if not managed.

3. **Insect Damage**:
   - **Scale Insects**: These can cause spotting and a decline in leaf health. The feeding of these insects often leaves behind honeydew, which can encourage sooty mold growth.
   - **Leaf Miners**: These insects create tunnels within the leaf tissue, causing spotting and discoloration.

4. **Environmental Stress**:
   - **Drought Stress**: Lack of water can cause leaves to develop spots and dry out. Check if the trees have been receiving adequate water, especially during dry periods.
   - **Nutrient Deficiency**: Imbalance in essential nutrients can cause various symptoms, including spotting on the leaves.

### Diagnosis and Management:

1. **Close Inspection**:
   - **Check Undersides**: Look for signs of fungal spores, insect activity, or any other visible clues on the underside of the leaves.
   - **Pattern of Damage**: Note if the damage is uniform or concentrated in certain areas, which can help narrow down the cause.

2. **Cultural Practices**:
   - **Watering**: Ensure the trees are well-watered, especially during dry spells.
   - **Sanitation**: Remove and destroy affected leaves to prevent the spread of pathogens.
   - **Mulching**: Apply mulch around the base of the trees to retain soil moisture and provide a buffer against temperature extremes.

3. **Chemical Treatments**:
   - **Fungicides**: If a fungal infection is suspected, a fungicide labeled for use on Live Oaks may be applied. Ensure to follow all label directions.
   - **Insecticides**: For insect infestations, use appropriate insecticides. For scale insects, horticultural oils or systemic insecticides can be effective.

4. **Professional Help**:
