2. **Integrated Pest Management (IPM)**: While IPM is a component of PHC, it&#x27;s crucial to emphasize that PHC shifts the focus from merely controlling pests to enhancing the overall health and resilience of plants. This involves monitoring plants for signs of distress, identifying potential pests at their threshold levels, and intervening in a way that is sensitive to the environment and the specific needs of each plant (Landscape Business).

3. **Soil Management and Restoration**: A significant part of PHC is nurturing the soil, which is the foundation of plant health. Techniques such as soil aeration using tools like an AirSpade, incorporation of organic matter through compost and biochar, and careful management of soil moisture and nutrition can revitalize soil structure and function, fostering a supportive environment for plant roots (Tree Care Industry Magazine).
