---

**ASSISTANT**:
Major signs of dehydration in a four-month-old can include:

- Fewer wet diapers (less than six in 24 hours)
- Dry mouth and tongue
- Crying without tears
- Sunken soft spot (anterior fontanelle) on the head
- Sunken eyes and cheeks
- Lethargy or irritability
- Skin that doesn&#x27;t bounce back when pinched and released

If your child is showing any of these signs, especially after having a stomach virus and diarrhea for several days, it is important to seek medical attention immediately. Dehydration in infants can progress rapidly and can be serious.
