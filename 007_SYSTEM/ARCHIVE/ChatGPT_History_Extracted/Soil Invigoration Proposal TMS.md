### **Background &amp; Objectives**  
The goal of this project is to restore soil health and long-term resilience in the critical root zones (CRZ) of **eight trees** impacted by recent construction activities. This treatment will improve soil structure, microbial activity, and nutrient availability to ensure continued tree health and vitality.  

---

### **Scope of Work**  

1. **Soil Invigoration (Air Spade Work):**  
   - Decompact and aerate soil to a depth of 6–12 inches.  
   - Incorporate **biochar**, **Tiger Sulfur**, and fertilizer into the soil profile to promote root health.  

2. **Compost Application:**  
   - Apply a 1&quot; layer of compost evenly across the treated area (~3,150 sq. ft.) to improve organic matter and microbial activity.  

3. **Mulch Application:**  
   - Spread 3&quot; of high-quality mulch to retain moisture, regulate soil temperature, and suppress weeds.  

4. **Fertilizer Options:**  
   - **Conventional Fertilizer (14-7-7):** Immediate, balanced nutrient availability.  
   - **Organic Fertilizer (MicroLife 6-2-4):**  
     - Builds soil health long-term with slow-release nutrients.  
     - Contains billions of beneficial microorganisms, endo/ecto mycorrhizae, and natural fortifications like kelp and humates.  

5. **Optional – Biostimulant Drench Application:**  
   - 1 gallon per tree of Soil Grower’s proprietary biostimulant formula.  
   - **Benefits:**  
     - Enhances root growth and nutrient uptake.  
     - Increases stress resistance and microbial activity.  
   - **Cost:** $150/tree ($1,200 total for 8 trees).  

**Note:** The biostimulant drench and organic fertilizer are materials Soil Grower has on-hand. The cost of these materials and the associated labor will go directly to Soil Grower.
