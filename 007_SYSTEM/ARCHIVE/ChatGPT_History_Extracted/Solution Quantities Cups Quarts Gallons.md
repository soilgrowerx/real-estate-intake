# Solution Quantities: Cups, Quarts, Gallons

**USER**:
If I have a solution that calls for a quarter cup per gallon and I have 180 gallons how much solution do I need give me the quantity in cups quarts and gallons

---

**ASSISTANT**:
To determine the amount of solution needed for 180 gallons when the recipe calls for a quarter cup per gallon, follow these steps:

1. **Calculate the amount in cups:**
   - Amount per gallon = 0.25 cups
   - Total gallons = 180 gallons
   - Total amount in cups = 0.25 cups/gallon * 180 gallons
   \[
   \text{Total amount in cups} = 0.25 \times 180 = 45 \text{ cups}
   \]

2. **Convert cups to quarts:**
   - There are 4 cups in a quart.
   - Total amount in quarts = Total amount in cups / 4
   \[
   \text{Total amount in quarts} = \frac{45}{4} = 11.25 \text{ quarts}
   \]

3. **Convert quarts to gallons:**
   - There are 4 quarts in a gallon.
   - Total amount in gallons = Total amount in quarts / 4
   \[
   \text{Total amount in gallons} = \frac{11.25}{4} = 2.8125 \text{ gallons}
   \]

Therefore, for 180 gallons, you will need:

- **45 cups**
- **11.25 quarts**
- **2.8125 gallons**
