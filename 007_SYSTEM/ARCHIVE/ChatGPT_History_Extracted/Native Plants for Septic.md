**ASSISTANT**:
For a septic system, especially in the Edwards Plateau region with its heavy limestone and calcium carbonate deposits, you&#x27;ll want to select native plants that have non-invasive root systems and can tolerate both drought and high calcium soil. These plants should be able to thrive in Central Texas and not interfere with the septic system. Here is a comprehensive list of native, non-invasive plants that are suitable for planting around septic tanks and leach fields in your area:

### 1. **Grasses and Ground Covers**
   - **Buffalo Grass (Bouteloua dactyloides)**: A drought-tolerant native grass that grows well in full sun and requires minimal maintenance.
   - **Blue Grama (Bouteloua gracilis)**: Another drought-tolerant grass, great for stabilizing soil and adding a natural look.
   - **Sideoats Grama (Bouteloua curtipendula)**: A native bunchgrass that provides erosion control and handles the alkaline soil well.
   - **Curly Mesquite Grass (Hilaria belangeri)**: A warm-season, low-growing grass that can handle dry conditions.
   - **Horseherb (Calyptocarpus vialis)**: A native ground cover that thrives in shaded or sunny areas and spreads easily.

### 2. **Wildflowers and Perennials**
   - **Blackfoot Daisy (Melampodium leucanthum)**: A drought-tolerant perennial that blooms in white and thrives in rocky soil.
   - **Mealy Blue Sage (Salvia farinacea)**: A native sage with blue flowers that attracts pollinators and requires minimal water.
   - **Engelmann Daisy (Engelmannia peristenia)**: A bright, drought-resistant perennial that thrives in full sun.
   - **Damianita (Chrysactinia mexicana)**: An evergreen, low-growing shrub with yellow blooms and aromatic foliage, perfect for dry conditions.
   - **Purple Coneflower (Echinacea purpurea)**: A pollinator-friendly wildflower that can handle rocky and alkaline soil.
   - **Gregg&#x27;s Mistflower (Conoclinium greggii)**: A perennial that attracts butterflies, especially the Queen butterfly, and grows well in sunny areas.

### 3. **Shrubs**
   - **Flame Acanthus (Anisacanthus quadrifidus var. wrightii)**: A deciduous shrub with orange-red flowers, highly attractive to hummingbirds and adaptable to dry soils.
   - **Rock Rose (Pavonia lasiopetala)**: A drought-tolerant shrub with pink flowers that can handle rocky and limestone-rich soil.
   - **Texas Sage (Leucophyllum frutescens)**: Also known as &quot;cenizo,&quot; this shrub thrives in well-drained, alkaline soil and blooms after rain.
   - **Elbow Bush (Forestiera pubescens)**: A native shrub with fragrant yellow flowers in early spring, tolerant of limestone soil and dry conditions.
   - **Agarita (Mahonia trifoliolata)**: A spiny, evergreen shrub with yellow flowers and red berries, perfect for limestone soils.

### 4. **Herbs and Aromatics**
   - **Cedar Sage (Salvia roemeriana)**: A shade-tolerant sage that blooms bright red and is native to the Edwards Plateau.
   - **Mexican Mint Marigold (Tagetes lucida)**: A drought-tolerant herb that can handle rocky soil and produces yellow flowers in the fall.
   - **Lemon Beebalm (Monarda citriodora)**: An aromatic herb that attracts pollinators and grows well in dry, rocky soil.

### 5. **Trees and Small Woody Plants**
   - **Escarpment Live Oak (Quercus fusiformis)**: A smaller variety of live oak native to Central Texas, suitable for planting farther away from the septic system.
   - **Texas Redbud (Cercis canadensis var. texensis)**: A small ornamental tree that grows well in alkaline soils and adds vibrant spring color.
   - **Mexican Buckeye (Ungnadia speciosa)**: A small tree that produces pink flowers and thrives in well-drained limestone soils.

### **Planting Tips**
- **Root Depth**: Avoid planting any species with deep, invasive roots that could penetrate or damage septic tanks or drain fields.
- **Location**: Keep larger shrubs and trees at least 10-20 feet away from septic tanks and leach fields to avoid root intrusion.
- **Soil**: Amend the soil minimally, as native plants are adapted to the local soil conditions and perform best without significant alterations.
- **Watering**: Use drought-tolerant plants that require minimal watering to avoid over-saturating the soil near the septic system.
