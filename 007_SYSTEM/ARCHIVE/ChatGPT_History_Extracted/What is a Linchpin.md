# What is a Linchpin

**USER**:
What is a linchpin

---

**ASSISTANT**:
A *linchpin* is a term used to describe something or someone essential for holding a system, organization, or process together. Originally, a linchpin referred to a pin that secured a wheel to an axle, preventing the wheel from coming off and ensuring the vehicle could function properly.

In a figurative sense, a linchpin is a key player or component that is crucial to the success or stability of a situation. For instance, in a company, a linchpin might be an employee whose unique skills or leadership are indispensable. In broader contexts, it can refer to any central element without which the entire structure or system would fail or be severely compromised.
