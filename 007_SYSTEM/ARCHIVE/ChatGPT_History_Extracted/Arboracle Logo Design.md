How many classes of plant hormones are there?

---

**ASSISTANT**:
There are generally five classic classes of plant hormones known as phytohormones:

1. **Auxins** - Involved in the regulation of plant growth processes.
2. **Cytokinins** - Promote cell division and growth, and delay the aging of leaves.
3. **Gibberellins** - Important for seed germination, stem elongation, and flowering.
4. **Ethylene** - Influences fruit ripening and leaf fall (abscission).
5. **Abscisic acid (ABA)** - Plays a key role in seed dormancy and environmental stress responses.

In addition to these, there are other groups of plant hormones that have been recognized:

6. **Brassinosteroids** - Similar to animal steroid hormones, they play roles in many plant growth and developmental processes.
7. **Jasmonates** - Involved in plant responses to stress and also in developmental processes.
8. **Salicylic acid** - Important in the plant&#x27;s defense response against pathogens.
9. **Strigolactones** - Involve in regulating shoot branching and other developmental processes.

These hormones interact in complex networks to regulate growth, development, and responses to environmental stimuli.

Painting fresh cuts on oak trees, particularly in areas prone to Oak Wilt, is a practice meant to prevent the spread of the disease. Oak Wilt is caused by a fungus and is spread by insects and through root grafts between trees.

Here&#x27;s why and how it&#x27;s done:

- **Why:** Sap-feeding beetles are attracted to the fresh wounds of oak trees and can carry Oak Wilt spores from infected trees to the fresh cuts, infecting a healthy tree. Painting the wounds immediately after cutting can help prevent these beetles from infecting the tree.
  
- **Best Time:** The best time to prune oaks to minimize the risk of Oak Wilt infection is during the tree&#x27;s dormant season, which is typically in the cold months of the year when insects are least active. In many regions, this would be from late fall to early spring.

- **Best Practices:** Use a pruning paint or wound dressing specifically designed for use on trees. It&#x27;s important to only cover the wound, not the surrounding bark, as this can harm the tree. Also, you should only apply a thin layer; don&#x27;t over-apply the product. 

- **Technique:** The technique involves cleaning the pruning tools between cuts to prevent the spread of disease, making proper cuts to promote healing, and then immediately applying the tree wound dressing to the fresh cuts.

Please note that the use of wound dressings is subject to some debate among arborists. Some argue that it&#x27;s not necessary or even beneficial in many cases. It&#x27;s essential to follow regional guidelines and recommendations, as practices can vary depending on the local prevalence of diseases like Oak Wilt.
