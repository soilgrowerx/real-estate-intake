Oxygen (O) on the sides: Formal charge = 6 (valence electrons) - 6 (non-bonding electrons) - 1/2(4) (shared electrons) = -1
Step 2. Evaluate the formal charges to determine the most stable arrangement:

The &quot;OSO&quot; arrangement has a formal charge of -1 on each oxygen atom.
The &quot;SOO&quot; arrangement also has a formal charge of -1 on each oxygen atom.
Step 3. Compare the formal charges:

Since both arrangements result in the same formal charges on the oxygen atoms, we need to consider other factors.

Consider electronegativity and resonance:

In sulfur dioxide, oxygen is more electronegative than sulfur, so it is preferable for oxygen atoms to have negative formal charges rather than sulfur. Based on formal charge considerations and resonance, the correct arrangement of atoms in sulfur dioxide is likely &quot;OSO.&quot;
