For a 2013 Toyota 4Runner, considering cost-effectiveness and safety for a first-time teenage driver, here are some of the best tire options:

1. **Yokohama Geolandar A/T G015**:
   - **Pros**: Great all-terrain performance, reliable wet and dry traction, solid light-snow capabilities, and quiet operation.
   - **Cons**: Can feel harsh over repetitive impacts.
   - **Warranty**: 60,000 miles for P-metric sizes, 50,000 miles for LT-metric sizes.
   - **Overall**: This tire is versatile and performs well on both highways and off-road, making it an excellent choice for varied driving conditions【9†source】【12†source】.

2. **General Grabber HTS 60**:
   - **Pros**: Competitively priced, excellent high-speed stability, good wet and dry traction, and a 65,000-mile treadwear warranty.
   - **Cons**: Not the best performer on snow.
   - **Overall**: A solid budget-friendly option that balances performance and durability, ideal for daily driving with occasional off-road use【9†source】【11†source】.

3. **Firestone Destination LE3**:
   - **Pros**: Well-balanced handling, robust traction in dry and wet conditions, comfortable and quiet ride, and a class-leading 70,000-mile warranty.
   - **Cons**: Less refined on rough asphalt, lacks snow performance compared to premium rivals.
   - **Overall**: A very durable and cost-effective tire, suitable for highway driving and light towing【12†source】.

4. **Falken WildPeak A/T3W**:
   - **Pros**: Excellent traction and handling on both paved and unpaved roads, innovative sidewall design to prevent stone retention, and good high-speed stability.
   - **Cons**: Some highway noise.
   - **Overall**: This all-terrain tire offers a good balance between on-road comfort and off-road capability, making it a versatile choice for different driving environments【10†source】【11†source】.

Each of these tires has strengths that cater to different driving needs and conditions. For a teenage driver, it’s crucial to prioritize safety, durability, and all-weather performance. The Yokohama Geolandar A/T G015 and General Grabber HTS 60 stand out for their balanced performance and affordability, making them excellent choices for a new driver.
