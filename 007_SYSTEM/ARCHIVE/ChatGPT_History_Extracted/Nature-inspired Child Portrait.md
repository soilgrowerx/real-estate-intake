Polyembryony refers to the phenomenon where two or more embryos develop from a single fertilized egg. In plants, this can result from multiple embryo sacs in the ovule or from the division of a single embryo sac. Plants with polyembryonic seeds are often found in the citrus family, where it is not uncommon for a single seed to produce more than one seedling.
