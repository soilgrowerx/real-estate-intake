**Step 2: Identify the Formula**

The general formula for exponential growth is given by:
\[ P = P_0 \times (1 + r)^t \]
Where:
- \( P \) is the final population.
- \( P_0 \) is the initial population (500 bacteria, in this case).
- \( r \) is the rate of growth per unit of time (doubling means \( r = 1 \), or 100%).

- \( P_0 = 500 \)

6.84 The ionic radii of the ions S2–, Cl–, and K+ are 184, 181, 138 pm respectively. Explain why these ions have different sizes even though they contain the same number of electrons.


Although the ions S2-, Cl-, and K+ have the same number of electrons, they exhibit different sizes due to variations in their effective nuclear charge and electron configuration. When an atom gains or loses electrons to form ions, its effective nuclear charge changes. This effective charge is the attraction felt by the outermost electrons towards the nucleus and is influenced by the number of protons in the nucleus and the number of inner electrons shielding the outer electrons from the nucleus&#x27;s positive charge. In the case of S2-, since it gains two electrons, there is greater electron-electron repulsion, causing the electrons to spread out further, resulting in a larger ionic radius. On the other hand, Cl- gains only one electron, but since it has more protons in its nucleus than S2-, the effective nuclear charge is stronger, pulling the electrons closer to the nucleus, leading to a smaller ionic radius. K+, having lost an electron, experiences a decrease in electron-electron repulsion and a stronger effective nuclear charge compared to S2-, resulting in a smaller ionic radius than S2- but larger than Cl-. Therefore, although these ions contain the same number of electrons, their different effective nuclear charges and electron configurations lead to variations in their sizes.
