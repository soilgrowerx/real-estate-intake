To calculate the total revenue for Year 2018 in cell F4, you need to sum the values from quarters 1 to 4 for that year. You can achieve this by using the SUM function in Excel. Here is the formula you should enter in cell F4:

```excel
=SUM(B4:E4)
```

This formula adds up the values in cells B4, C4, D4, and E4, giving you the total revenue for Year 2018.

To change the page margins to &quot;Wide&quot; or set the top, left, bottom, and right margins to 1 inch in Excel, follow these steps:

1. **Go to the Page Layout Tab:**
   Click on the `Page Layout` tab on the Ribbon.

2. **Open the Margins Menu:**
   In the `Page Setup` group, click on the `Margins` button to open the drop-down menu.

3. **Select Wide Margins:**
   - From the drop-down menu, choose `Wide`. This option sets the top, left, bottom, and right margins to 1 inch.

Alternatively, you can manually set each margin to 1 inch:

1. **Open the Page Setup Dialog:**
   Click on the small arrow at the bottom-right corner of the `Page Setup` group to open the Page Setup dialog box.

2. **Set the Margins:**
   - Go to the `Margins` tab.
   - Set the `Top`, `Bottom`, `Left`, and `Right` margins to 1 inch.
   - Click `OK` to apply the changes.

These steps will adjust the page margins as required.

What is wrong with my blueberry bush? It was just fine a week ago and now all of a sudden is wilted it seems like it is dying

**ASSISTANT**:
From the images provided, it appears that your blueberry bush is experiencing some form of stress. Here are some potential causes and solutions:

### Potential Causes:

1. **Watering Issues:**
   - **Overwatering or Underwatering:** Blueberry bushes are sensitive to both overwatering and underwatering. Ensure the soil is well-draining and that the plant is neither sitting in water nor completely dried out.
   - **Solution:** Check the moisture level of the soil regularly. Water the plant deeply, but allow the soil to dry slightly between waterings.

2. **Nutrient Deficiency:**
   - **Yellowing leaves can indicate a lack of essential nutrients such as nitrogen, iron, or magnesium.**
   - **Solution:** Use a fertilizer specifically formulated for acid-loving plants like blueberries. Consider performing a soil test to check pH and nutrient levels.

3. **Pest or Disease:**
   - **Pests:** Inspect the leaves and stems for signs of pests such as aphids, mites, or caterpillars.
   - **Diseases:** Fungal infections or root rot can cause wilting and yellowing.
   - **Solution:** If pests are present, treat with appropriate insecticidal soap or neem oil. For fungal diseases, consider using a fungicide or improving air circulation around the plant.

4. **Environmental Stress:**
   - **Heat Stress:** High temperatures and direct sunlight can cause wilting and leaf damage.
   - **Solution:** Provide some shade during the hottest part of the day if temperatures are extremely high.

5. **Soil pH:**
   - **Blueberries require acidic soil (pH 4.5 to 5.5). A pH that is too high can cause nutrient uptake issues.**
   - **Solution:** Test the soil pH and amend it with sulfur or other acidifying agents if necessary.

### Immediate Steps:

1. **Inspect the Plant:**
   - Look for signs of pests, disease, or physical damage.

2. **Check Soil Moisture:**
   - Ensure the soil is not too wet or too dry.

3. **Adjust Watering:**
   - Modify your watering schedule based on the soil moisture level.

4. **Fertilize:**
   - Apply a balanced fertilizer suitable for blueberries if a nutrient deficiency is suspected.

5. **Provide Shade:**
   - If the plant is in direct sunlight and temperatures are high, provide temporary shade.

By addressing these factors, you should be able to determine the cause of the stress and take appropriate action to help your blueberry bush recover.
