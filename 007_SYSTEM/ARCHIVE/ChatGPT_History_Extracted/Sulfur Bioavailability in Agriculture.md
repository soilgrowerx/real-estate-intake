Sulfur is crucial for plant growth, aiding in the production of amino acids, proteins, enzymes, and vitamins. It also plays a vital role in chlorophyll formation and oxygen production during photosynthesis.

### Bioavailable Sources of Elemental Sulfur

1. **Elemental Sulfur Powder or Granules**
   - **Description**: This is one of the most direct sources of sulfur. It’s typically applied to the soil where soil bacteria oxidize it into sulfates that plants can absorb.
   - **Bioavailability**: The bioavailability of elemental sulfur depends significantly on soil conditions, particularly temperature and moisture, as these influence microbial activity. It might take time for sulfur to become available to plants, so it’s often applied well before planting.

2. **Gypsum (Calcium Sulfate)**
   - **Description**: Gypsum is a widely used sulfur source in agriculture, providing both calcium and sulfur in a form readily available to plants.
   - **Bioavailability**: Gypsum dissolves slowly, releasing sulfate ions that plants can immediately absorb, making it an excellent choice for providing a sustained supply of sulfur.

3. **Sulfate of Potash (Potassium Sulfate)**
   - **Description**: Besides potassium, this fertilizer also offers sulfate, delivering two essential nutrients.
   - **Bioavailability**: The sulfate in sulfate of potash is highly soluble and readily available to plants, making it an efficient source of sulfur.

4. **Ammonium Sulfate**
   - **Description**: This fertilizer combines nitrogen and sulfur in a form that is readily usable by plants.
   - **Bioavailability**: Ammonium sulfate quickly releases sulfate ions upon application, which plants can absorb, thus providing an immediate source of sulfur.

5. **Manures and Organic Composts**
   - **Description**: Organic sources like manure or composted plant material contain sulfur in organic forms.
   - **Bioavailability**: Sulfur from organic sources becomes available to plants as the material decomposes, a process influenced by microbial activity in the soil.

### Factors Influencing Sulfur Bioavailability

- **Soil pH**: Sulfur oxidation and the resultant sulfate availability are higher in neutral to slightly acidic soils.
- **Temperature and Moisture**: Microbial activity, crucial for the oxidation of elemental sulfur to sulfate, increases with warm and moist soil conditions.
- **Organic Matter Content**: Soils rich in organic matter support a healthy microbial population which assists in the breakdown and assimilation of sulfur by plants.
