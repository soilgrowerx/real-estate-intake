# Epsom Salt and Sulfur

**USER**:
With cannabis plants have someone who added Epsom salt and hydrogen peroxide to kill pathogens I suppose the Epsom salt was for the magnesium would it make sense to add sulfur along with the Epsom salt for absorption?

---

**ASSISTANT**:
Yes, it could make sense to add sulfur along with Epsom salt when growing cannabis, but it depends on the specific needs of the plant and the condition of your soil or growing medium.

Epsom salt (magnesium sulfate) provides both magnesium and sulfur, which are both essential nutrients for cannabis plants. Magnesium is crucial for photosynthesis, while sulfur plays a role in protein synthesis and chlorophyll formation. If you&#x27;re using Epsom salt, you&#x27;re already supplying some sulfur, but if you&#x27;re concerned about the overall sulfur levels in your growing medium, you could consider adding more sulfur, depending on the specific balance of nutrients.

However, be cautious with sulfur supplementation because too much sulfur can acidify the soil or growing medium, potentially leading to nutrient lockout or imbalances. If your soil or growing medium already has sufficient sulfur, adding more may not be necessary. It would be best to test the pH and nutrient levels first to determine if additional sulfur is needed.

In summary, while adding sulfur could be beneficial for absorption and overall plant health, especially if you&#x27;re deficient in sulfur, it&#x27;s crucial to avoid overloading the soil. Balancing nutrient levels is key to healthy cannabis plant growth.

These plants are still rather small and in the greening phase would it make sense to add black strap molasses when the plants start flowering when is it the best time to add it?

---

**ASSISTANT**:
Blackstrap molasses is often used during the flowering phase of cannabis growth to provide a rich source of carbohydrates, trace minerals, and nutrients like iron, calcium, magnesium, and potassium. The carbohydrates help feed beneficial microorganisms in the soil, promoting healthier root systems and aiding nutrient uptake, which is particularly important during flowering when the plant&#x27;s energy demands increase.

Here’s how you can time the use of blackstrap molasses:

1. **Early Flowering (Pre-flower)**: Start adding molasses when the plant begins to show signs of flowering (usually within the first two weeks of the flowering phase). At this stage, it helps the plant transition from vegetative growth to focusing on bud production. Using a diluted solution (e.g., 1 tablespoon per gallon of water) will help avoid overwhelming the plant early on.

2. **Mid to Late Flowering**: During this time, the plant will be focusing on producing buds, and its need for carbohydrates increases. Continue using molasses once a week or as part of your regular feeding schedule to maximize nutrient uptake and encourage larger, more resinous flowers. Make sure you&#x27;re not overfeeding, as this can lead to nutrient lockout or excessive soil buildup.

3. **Flushing**: Some growers stop using molasses in the final week or two before harvest when they begin to flush the plants with plain water to remove any excess nutrients and improve the flavor of the buds.

Using blackstrap molasses during the flowering phase, starting early and continuing into mid-flower, can enhance bud production and overall plant health, but like any amendment, moderation is key. Always monitor the plants’ response and adjust accordingly.
