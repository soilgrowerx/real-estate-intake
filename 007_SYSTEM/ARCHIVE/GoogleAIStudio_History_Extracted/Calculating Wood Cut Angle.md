### Method 1: The Scribing Method (No Special Tools Needed)

This is the classic, old-school way to get a perfect fit every time.

1.  **Position the Board:** Take your angled 2x4 and hold it in its final position, overlapping the vertical post. Make sure it's lying flat and aligned exactly where you want it.
2.  **Scribe the Line:** Take a pencil and use the face of the vertical support post as your guide. Slide the pencil down the face of that post, drawing a line directly onto your angled board.
3.  **Cut the Line:** That pencil line is the *exact* angle you need to cut. Take the board to your saw, align the blade carefully with the line, and make your cut.

### Method 2: Use a T-Bevel or Digital Angle Finder

This is the modern version of the scribing method and is great for repeatable cuts.

1.  **Set the Tool:** Place your T-bevel or digital angle finder into the corner formed by the horizontal beam and the vertical post.
2.  **Capture the Angle:** Hold the angled brace board in its final position. Adjust the angle finder so it sits flush against both the vertical post and the angled brace. This captures the true angle you need.
3.  **Transfer to the Saw:** Lock the T-bevel or note the degrees on your digital finder. Take it to your miter saw and adjust the saw's angle to match the tool perfectly.
4.  **Make a Test Cut:** Always cut a scrap piece first to test the fit before cutting your final board.
