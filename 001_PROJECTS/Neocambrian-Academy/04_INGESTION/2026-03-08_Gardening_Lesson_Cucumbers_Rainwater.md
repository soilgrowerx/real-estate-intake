# Gardening Lesson: Cucumber Cotyledons and Rainwater

## Summary
- **Cotyledon Leaves:** George explains the "catalidon" (cotyledon) leaf—the first two leaves attached to the seed. These will fall off naturally as the cucumber plant matures and the true leaves grow from the center.
- **Vertical Growth:** The plan is for the cucumber plants to grow up the wire entryway, eventually hanging down over the garden path for easy picking and eating.
- **Rainwater Microbes:** George highlights that rainwater is superior to hose water because it contains living organisms (microbes) that travel through the clouds, providing biological value to the soil.

## Lesson Content
**Botanical Concept:** Cotyledons vs. True Leaves.
**Physical Growth:** Vertical trellising over garden entryways.
**Biological Concept:** Microbes in rainwater and their role in plant health.
**Interactive Moment:** Bodie and Evyn's plan to eat cucumbers straight from the vine.
