# Time Change Lesson: Spring Forward

## Summary
- The speakers discuss a recent time change, where the clock "sprung forward" from one o'clock to two o'clock in the afternoon.
- They explain that this happens annually, with "spring forward" in spring and "fall back" in fall/winter, affecting sleep schedules.
- The conversation concludes with a plan to get food, referencing a previous discussion about a "wolf" sound and a desire to eat.

## Lesson Content
**Concept:** Daylight Savings Time (Spring Forward).
**Duration:** 1 hour jump.
**Explanation:** 60 minutes = 1 hour. 60 seconds = 1 minute.
**Impact:** Losing an hour (spring), gaining an hour/sleeping in (fall/winter).
**Geography:** Mention of University of Oregon (Beaverton/Eugene context) and family in Oregon.
