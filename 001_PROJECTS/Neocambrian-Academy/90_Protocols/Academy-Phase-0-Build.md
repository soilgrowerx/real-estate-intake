#### **2. The Socratic Guide Protocol**

## **VII. THE SACRED BOUNDARIES: WHAT PHASE 0 IS NOT**

To protect the integrity of the Neocambrian vision, we must define the **negative space**—what we refuse.

### **We Refuse:**

1. **Standardized Testing.** There are no "benchmarks" to hit by age 5 except the ones we define (the Competency Map). We do not compare to "grade level."
2. **Screen Time as Pedagogy.** No iPads, no "educational apps." Screens are **cognitive junk food**. If a child cannot focus without a screen, the problem is the environment, not the child.
3. **Forced Socialization.** If a child wants to play alone, they play alone. We do not manufacture "friendships" through adult-directed group activities.
4. **Punishment-Based Discipline.** No time-outs, no "consequences" disconnected from natural cause-effect. If a child breaks a rule (e.g., runs past the boundary stones), the consequence is *natural* (we all stop playing and return to the classroom because safety is compromised). This is not punishment—it is reality.
5. **Parental Helicoptering.** Parents do not observe the Work Cycle or Sovereign Play. They drop off and pick up. The children need *sovereignty* from parental gaze. (Exception: Quarterly "Parent Observation Days" where parents watch from a distance but do not interact.)
6. **Artificial Acceleration.** We do not "push" children into reading/math/climbing before they are developmentally ready. The Montessori principle of "sensitive periods" is sacred. When the child is ready, they will *devour* the material. Until then, we wait.

### **We Embrace:**

1. **Mud, Dirt, and Mess.** A child who comes home clean did not learn today.
2. **Risk Within Reason.** Scraped knees and small burns (from touching a warm stone near the fire) are *education*, not failure.
3. **Silence.** Not all moments need adult narration. Sometimes the forest just *is*, and the child observes.
4. **Seasonal Rhythms.** In winter, we do less. In spring, we do more. We are not a factory. We are an organism.
5. **Multi-Age Learning.** Bodie (2.5) and Evyn (4) learn *from* each other. Evyn teaches Bodie patience. Bodie teaches Evyn wonder. This is the peer-teaching model that scales beyond nuclear families.
