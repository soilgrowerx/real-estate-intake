# Neocambrian Academy: Unit 2 Expanded - The Tree
**Theme**: Anatomy, Energy, and Interconnectedness.
**Target Age**: 3-5 Years

## I. Weekly Lesson Cycles

### Day 1: The Body of the Tree (Anatomy)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **The Tree Puzzle.** Present a 5-piece wooden puzzle (Root, Trunk, Branch, Leaf, Canopy). Use nomenclature cards to name each part.
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Anchor Tree Identification.** Find a single, large "Anchor Tree." Physically touch each part while naming it. Hug the trunk to feel the "bones" of the tree.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Interconnectedness* - Every part of the tree depends on the others to survive.

### Day 2: The Sunlight Feast (Energy)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Capillary Action Experiment.** Place celery stalks in water with red food coloring. Watch the "energy" travel up the stalk.
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Sun Catching.** Find a patch of sunlight hitting leaves. Discuss how the leaves are "eating" the light. Look for "sun-spots" on the forest floor.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Circularity* - Sunlight turns into sap, which builds the tree, which eventually returns to the earth.

### Day 3: Seasonal Clothes (Deciduous vs. Evergreen)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Sorting Baskets.** Sort real leaf and needle samples into "Deciduous" (Naked in Winter) and "Evergreen" (Always has Clothes).
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **The Winter Coat Hunt.** Walk the property and find one tree with "clothes on" and one with "clothes off." Feel the difference between a leaf and a needle.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Adaptation* - Trees change their clothes to survive the cold and wind.

### Day 4: The Hidden Half (Root Systems)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Root Mapping.** Use yarn to trace the spread of roots on a large paper diagram. Emphasize that roots spread as wide as the canopy.
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Root Discovery.** Find a tree with exposed surface roots. Gently clear away loose leaves to see where they lead. Discuss how roots "drink" and "anchor."
- **Constitutional Link**: *Interconnectedness* - To pull the root is to hurt the tree.

### Day 5: Tree as an Apartment (Habitats)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Habitat Matching.** Match animal cards (Squirrel, Woodpecker, Beetle, Rabbit) to their "floor" in the tree apartment (Canopy, Trunk, Roots).
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **The Apartment Inspection.** Use magnifying glasses to find "tenants" in the Anchor Tree (spiderwebs, holes, moss).
- **Constitutional Link**: *Intrinsic Value of Life* - The tree has value as a home for many, beyond just being a tree.

## III. Visual Aid Specifications

### 1. Simplified Photosynthesis Chart (V4)
- **Type**: A3 Infographic
- **Content**: A tree with three simple icons:
    - **Sun** -> "Energy In"
    - **Rain** -> "Water Up"
    - **Air** -> "Air In"
    - **Sap/Sugar** -> "Tree Food" (flowing down the trunk).
- **Purpose**: To explain energy conversion without complex biology.

### 2. The Hidden Half: Root Diagram (V5)
- **Type**: Cross-section Poster
- **Content**: Shows the visible tree above ground and a mirror-image, massive root network below ground.
- **Key Note**: Labels showing the "Drip Line" (where rain falls from the leaves to the roots).
- **Purpose**: To visualize the tree's invisible foundation.

### 3. Deciduous vs. Evergreen Contrast (V6)
- **Type**: Dual-sided Reference Card
- **Content**:
    - **Side A**: A bare Oak tree in snow ("The Sleeper").
    - **Side B**: A green Pine tree in snow ("The Worker").
- **Purpose**: To teach seasonal adaptation.
