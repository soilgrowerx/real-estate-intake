# Neocambrian Academy: Unit 1 Expanded - The Forest Floor
**Theme**: Decomposition, Worms, and the Mycelial Web.
**Target Age**: 3-5 Years

## I. Weekly Lesson Cycles

### Day 1: The Earth's Skin (Soil Texture)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Montessori Soil Sorting.** Present three jars containing dry Sand, Silt, and Clay. Use nomenclature cards to name each. Child uses a pincer grip to transfer small amounts into a sorting tray.
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Soil Alchemy.** Children collect a handful of local dirt. Add drops of water. Rub between fingers to determine: Does it feel gritty (Sand), smooth (Silt), or sticky (Clay)?
- **Constitutional Link**: *Interconnectedness* - The texture of the soil determines what can grow above it.

### Day 2: Underworld Architects (Worms & Life)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Worm Life Cycle.** Introduction of 3-Part Cards (Egg, Hatchling, Juvenile, Adult). Discuss how worms "eat" the earth to make it healthy.
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **The Worm Tunnel Hunt.** Gently roll over a "safe" log or stone. Observe the tunnels. Can we find a "Master Architect"? Discuss how they breathe through their skin.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Circularity* - Worms turn old leaves into new soil.

### Day 3: The Great Recyclers (Decomposition)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Decomposition Sorting.** A tray with various items (Apple core, Leaf, Plastic spoon, Metal bolt). Child sorts into "Returns to Earth" vs. "Stays Forever."
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **The Decomposition Station.** Designate a 2x2 foot area in the Garden. Add the "Returns to Earth" items. Observe daily how the forest begins to "eat" them.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Circularity* - There is no "away." Everything goes somewhere.

### Day 4: The Mycelial Web (The Forest's Nervous System)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Mapping the Web.** Present a diagram of a tree with a white, thread-like network connecting the roots. Use white yarn to connect "Tree Cards" on the rug.
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Root Tracing.** Find an exposed tree root. Trace it with a finger until it disappears into the earth. Imagine the white threads (mycelium) talking to the next tree.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Interconnectedness* - To pull the root is to hurt the tree.

### Day 5: Synthesis & Review
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **The Soil Profile Jar.** Child layers Sand, Silt, and Clay in a clear jar, adds water, and shakes. Watch them settle into the "Skin of the Earth."
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Micro-Habitat Sit.** Sit silently for 5 minutes inside a hula-hoop perimeter on the forest floor. What is moving? What is changing?
- **Constitutional Link**: *Adaptation* - Observe how the forest floor handles the morning dew or a passing breeze.

## III. Visual Aid Specifications

### 1. The Soil Profile (V1)
- **Type**: Large Wall Chart / A3
- **Content**: A vertical slice of the earth.
- **Layers**: 
    - **O-Horizon**: Green grass and dark brown Humus (The "Skin").
    - **A-Horizon**: Topsoil (where the worms live).
    - **B-Horizon**: Subsoil (lighter color, more clay).
    - **C-Horizon**: Parent Material (Broken rocks).
- **Purpose**: To show the child that the ground has depth and history.

### 2. Worm Life Cycle (V2)
- **Type**: 3-Part Nomenclature Cards
- **Images**: High-fidelity macro photos of: Cocoon (Egg), Hatchling, Juvenile (no clitellum), Adult (with clitellum).
- **Labels**: "Egg", "Baby Worm", "Young Worm", "Mother Earth Worm".

### 3. The Mycelial Web (V3)
- **Type**: Transparent Overlay or Infographic
- **Content**: Two trees side-by-side. Above ground they look separate. Below ground, a glowing white web (Mycelium) connects their roots.
- **Key Note**: Include small icons of "messages" (sugar, warnings) moving along the web.
- **Purpose**: To visualize the invisible connections of the holobiont.
