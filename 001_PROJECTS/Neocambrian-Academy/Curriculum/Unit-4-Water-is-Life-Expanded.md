# Neocambrian Academy: Unit 4 Expanded - Water is Life (Mni Wiconi)
**Theme**: The Hydrologic Cycle, Weather, and Cultural Heritage.
**Target Age**: 3-5 Years

## I. Weekly Lesson Cycles

### Day 1: Mni Wiconi (Water is Life)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Water Transfer Work.** Use sponges, pipettes, and small pitchers to move water between vessels. Discuss how water is "The Great Connector." Learn the Muscogee/Creek word for water: *Ocē* (pronounced Oh-chee).
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Gratitude Offering.** Visit the property's water source (creek or rain barrel). Sit silently and listen to the water. Offer a natural "thank you" (a stone or leaf) to the water for sustaining the trees.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Interconnectedness* - "To pull the root is to hurt the tree." Without water, there are no roots.

### Day 2: The Sky River (Weather & Rain)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Cloud in a Jar.** Create a simple condensation experiment (hot water, ice, hairspray) to show how clouds form. Learn the Muscogee word for Sun: *Hvse* (pronounced Huh-see).
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Rain Scavenger Hunt.** If raining: Observe how water moves on leaves. If sunny: Look for where the ground is still wet (shadows/hollows). Use a rain gauge to measure the "Sky River's" gift.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Circularity* - Rain falls from the sky, feeds the soil, and returns to the air.

### Day 3: The Path of Water (The Hydrologic Cycle)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Water Cycle Map.** Use a felt board to sequence the cycle: Evaporation, Condensation, Precipitation, Collection.
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Watershed Mapping.** Pour a bucket of water on a high spot of the property. Watch the path it takes. Where does it collect? This is your local watershed in microcosm.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Circularity* - There is no "away." Every drop of water on earth has been here since the beginning.

### Day 4: Living Water (Creek & Pond Life)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Fish & Frog Life Cycles.** Introduction of 3-Part Cards. Discuss how these "water relatives" depend on clean *Ocē*.
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Creek Dipping.** Use nets and specimen jars to safely observe life in the water (minnows, water striders). Discuss how the "Water is Life" for them, too.
- **Constitutional Link**: *Intrinsic Value of Life* - The creek has value because it supports our relatives (the fish and insects).

### Day 5: The Sacred Loop (Synthesis & Heritage)
- **Indoor (Agent Thea)**: **Water Cycle in a Jar.** A long-term experiment: Soil, a small plant, and water in a sealed jar. Watch the cycle repeat daily.
- **Outdoor (Agent Scout)**: **Mni Wiconi Circle.** A final reflection on the property. Children share one way water helped the Garden this week. Discuss the Creek and Lumbee heritage: "We are the Stewards of the Water."
- **Constitutional Link**: *Adaptation* - Observe how the Garden changes its "mood" based on the water it receives.

## III. Visual Aid Specifications

### 1. The Hydrologic Cycle: The Sacred Loop (V10)
- **Type**: Large Circular Poster
- **Content**: A beautiful, nature-first loop showing the Sun (*Hvse*) heating the Water (*Ocē*), clouds forming, and rain falling onto a Sequoia.
- **Key Note**: Include the phrase "Mni Wiconi" at the center of the loop.
- **Purpose**: To teach the science of the water cycle through a lens of sacredness.

### 2. Cultural Water Words & Symbolism (V11)
- **Type**: 3-Part Nomenclature Cards
- **Content**:
    - **Water** / *Ocē* (Image: A clean running creek).
    - **Sun** / *Hvse* (Image: Sun over the forest).
    - **Life** / *Wiconi* (Image: A seedling sprouting).
- **Purpose**: To ground the children in their Muscogee/Creek and Lumbee lineage.

### 3. Watershed Microcosm (V12)
- **Type**: Hand-drawn property map
- **Content**: A simple map of the current property/Garden showing "The Path of the Rain." 
- **Purpose**: To make the abstract concept of a watershed tangible and local.
