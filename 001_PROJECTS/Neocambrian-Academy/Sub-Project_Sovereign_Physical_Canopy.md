# Sub-Project: Sovereign Physical Canopy Integration

**Context:** The physical architecture and layout of the Neocambrian Academy must reflect and enforce the 200-year legacy of biological symbiosis, while ensuring all digital tracking (via Arboracle) is sovereign and aligned exclusively with the child's best interests.

## Core Directives

1. **Dendritic Flow:** Abandon rectilinear grids for campus layout. Movement mimics water or root systems—branching paths that gather students in functional nodes rather than forcing them down artificial hallways.
2. **Respirational Spaces:** The architecture must support the natural respiratory cycle of learning: an in-breath of sheltered, focused observation, followed by an out-breath of physical, unstructured exploration in the soil.
3. **Material Symbiosis:** Structures must emerge directly from the local environment (rammed earth walls, living roofs, natural timber). Buildings must breathe and age visibly, teaching thermal mass and biological decay patterns.
4. **The Arboracle Integration (Sovereign Tracking):** Students bind to the digital mycelium using Bodhi and the Arboracle platform, becoming stewards of the campus canopy. They track ecological benefits (water retention, soil stability, carbon mapping) of their physical space.
5. **Deep-Time Stewardship:** The curriculum is anchored in a 200-year legacy. The youngest students plant saplings that form the architectural canopy for future cohorts.

## Foundational Constraint: Sovereignty

> "As long as the tracking is truly sovereign and done in the child's best interests."

The integration of physical routines with digital tracking (via Arboracle) must rigorously adhere to the principle of digital sovereignty. Data collected by or about the students must not be commodified or exposed to external silos. The digital tools serve only to deepen the student's connection to the canopy and their own learning trajectory, acting as a sovereign extension of their own mind and stewardship, not as a surveillance mechanism.
