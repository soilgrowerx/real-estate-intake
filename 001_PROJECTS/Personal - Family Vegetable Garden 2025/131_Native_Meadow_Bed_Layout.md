# Native Garden Bed Design & Cardinal Layout

## The East Sun Anchor
* **Center (Woody Spine):** 2 Esperanza (Var. angustata) placed dead center, spaced exactly 5 feet apart.
* **North/South Flanks:** 2 Big Bluestem (Grass) planted strictly on the north and south flanks of the Esperanza to create a windbreak (3 feet away from woody stems).
* **Western Edge:** 2 Calylophus (compact) positioned on the western edge facing the house to block the harshest afternoon street heat (18 inches of clearance from the tall grasses).
* **Eastern/Southern Perimeters:** 4 Purple Skullcap alternating along the eastern and southern perimeters (spaced 2 feet apart).
* **Extreme Eastern Edge:** 4 Creeping Winecup interplanted with the Skullcap along the extreme eastern edge to spill toward the road (spaced 3 feet apart).

## The Mid Yard Meadow Sweep
* **Center (Vertical Focal Spire):** 1 Wild Bergamot dropped directly in the center with a dedicated 2-foot radius for maximum air circulation.
* **Northern Quadrant (Quarantine Zone):** 3 Gregg's Mistflower banished to the extreme northern quadrant (spaced 3 feet apart and a full 3 feet away from the Bergamot) so their aggressive rhizomes march away from the slower growers.
* **Eastern Face (Morning Sun):** 2 Purple Coneflower planted on the eastern face, spaced 18 inches apart.
* **Eastern Edge:** 2 Gulf Penstemon grouped tightly with the Coneflowers (spaced 18 inches apart).
* **Southern & Western Edges:** 5 Pink Skullcap forming a dense, unbroken border across the southern and western edges (spaced 18 inches apart).

## The Protected Understory
* **Western Foundation Wall:** 2 Turk's Cap buried right against the western foundation wall to block the punishing late afternoon sun (spaced 4 feet apart).
* **Eastern Side:** 2 TX Gold Columbine planted strictly on the eastern side of the Turk's Cap (spaced 18 inches apart, at least 2 feet away from the base of the Turk's Cap). They grab the morning rays and sit totally shielded by noon.

## Implementation Wisdom
* Score the root balls with a hori-hori knife before planting to force outward growth.
* Do not amend the holes with rich compost. Texas natives expect poor soil, and rich amendments encourage the roots to stay in the hole instead of pushing outward into the native dirt.
