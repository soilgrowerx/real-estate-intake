# 130 Red Bud Garden Wisdom

## Property Address
130 Red Bud, Liberty Hill, TX 78642

## Garden Layout & Wisdom

### East Garden (The Butterfly)
*   **Location:** Within 20 feet of the road.
*   **Shape:** A large Butterfly.
*   **Purpose:** Pollinator sanctuary and visual road buffer.

### Mid-Yard Meadow (The Spiral)
*   **Shape:** A Fibonacci-inspired spiral.
*   **Planting Pattern (Central to East/West):**
    *   **Center/Focus:** Bergamot (*Monarda fistulosa*).
    *   **East Side:** 2 Purple Coneflower (*Echinacea purpurea*) and Gulf Penstemon (*Penstemon tenuis*).
    *   **West Side:** 2 Purple Coneflower (*Echinacea purpurea*) and Gulf Penstemon (*Penstemon tenuis*).

## Plant Palette & Care

### Purple Coneflower (Echinacea purpurea)
*   **Wisdom:** A resilient pollinator-friendly wildflower. Handles rocky and alkaline Hill Country soil well.
*   **Maintenance:** Drought tolerant once established. Deadhead to encourage more blooms or leave seed heads for winter birds.

### Gulf Penstemon (Penstemon tenuis)
*   **Wisdom:** Spring bloomer that provides nectar for hummingbirds and bees.
*   **Maintenance:** Prefers well-drained soil. Cut back after blooming to maintain shape.

### Bergamot / Bee Balm (Monarda fistulosa)
*   **Wisdom:** High nectar value; the "heart" of the spiral.
*   **Maintenance:** Needs good air circulation to prevent powdery mildew. Prefers slightly more moisture than the penstemon but is Hill Country hardy.

## Note for Future Owners
This garden is designed as a "Living Game" of regeneration. The patterns (Butterfly and Spiral) are not just aesthetic; they are functional biological branching networks designed to maximize edge effect and pollinator interaction.
