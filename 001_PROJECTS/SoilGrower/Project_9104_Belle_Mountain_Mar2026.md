## Scope of Work Overview
- **Target:** 6 Live Oaks in the front yard, 2 Live Oaks on the south side of the house, and 2 Shin Oaks on the side by the pool.
- **Service:** Deep root soil invigoration via airspade (creating holes/pockets 4-8" deep) and packing them with pre-charged biochar blend (mycorrhizal fungi, sea minerals, horn manure compost, fermented).
- **Red Oaks (Back Property):** Manually apply/work in biochar by hand for the several Red Oaks located in the back part of the property.
- **Goal:** Improve soil structure, lock in nutrients, buffer high alkalinity/calcium, and increase resilience against Oak Wilt.
- **Root Flare Excavation:** Minor root flare exposure and pruning of any girdling/constricting roots on specific trees where soil is piled too high on the trunk.

## Site Notes & Observations
- **Tree Health:** Trees are generally looking okay, but canopy is thin. Client noted defoliation/thinning. Assessed as likely due to the heavy moisture from last year followed by stress, but overall resilient.
- **Red Oak / Oak Wilt Hazard:** Identified one Red Oak on the property that is struggling/hurt. **Recommendation:** Remove it entirely. It acts as a vector/entry point for beetles that cause Oak Wilt (creating sporulating mats). 
- **Shin Oaks:** Confirmed two Shin Oaks on site by the pool putting out a ton of root shoots/suckers. Client dislikes the shoots and the tannin stains from acorns on the driveway. Recommended keeping mulch heavy over them to suppress shoots, but acknowledged they will pop through. 
- **Landscape Integration:** Client is planning to rip out struggling grass (Bermuda/St. Augustine mix) and install native beds. Advised client that beds are better for trees than grass, especially on the Southern exposure side (heavy mulch). Recommended native seed mixes (like Buffalo grass) for remaining turf areas.
- **Septic System:** Property is on septic. Leech field is a raised mound. Need to maintain 15 ft distance from the leech field with equipment.
- **Shrubs/Understory:** Client removing boxwoods/hollies. Discussed Mountain Laurels (blooming heavily, likely due to rain). Advised knocking off dead stems but leaving green tops to push out new growth rather than topping them into "lollipops."
