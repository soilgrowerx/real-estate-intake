# Proposal to Tree Mann Solutions (TMS)

## Executive Summary
This proposal outlines a project aimed at restoring soil health and long-term resilience for eight trees at the Chamonix Condos Development Site. The project involves soil invigoration, compost application, mulch application, and fertilizer options to ensure tree vitality. It presents detailed costs, equipment needs, and a value proposition focusing on comprehensive tree care and sustainable practices. The proposal also proposes a revenue split that benefits Soil Grower's role in managing materials, equipment, labor, and logistics.

## Key Concepts & Taxonomies
* **Soil Health**: Refers to the condition of soil, including its ability to support plant life.
* **Compost Application**: The process of adding compost to soil to improve its structure and fertility.
* **Mulch Application**: The application of mulch to regulate soil temperature, retain moisture, and suppress weeds.
* **Fertilizer Options**: Choices between conventional and organic fertilizers for immediate or long-term nutrient availability.

## Actionable Takeaways & Core Claims
1. Soil invigoration and health restoration are critical for the long-term vitality of trees affected by construction activities.

## Raw Reading Notes
```markdown
### Background & Objectives
- Goal: Restore soil health and long-term resilience in the CRZ of eight trees.
- Treatment aims to improve soil structure, microbial activity, and nutrient availability.

### Scope of Work
1. Soil Invigoration:
   - Decompact and aerate soil (6–12 inches).
   - Incorporate biochar, Tiger Sulfur, and fertilizer into the soil profile.
2. Compost Application:
   - Apply 1" layer of compost (~3,150 sq. ft.).
3. Mulch Application:
   - Spread 3" of mulch to retain moisture, regulate temperature, and suppress weeds.
4. Fertilizer Options:
   - Conventional (14-7-7) for immediate nutrient availability.
   - Organic MicroLife 6-2-4 for long-term soil health.
5. Optional Biostimulant Drench Application:
   - Enhances root growth, nutrient uptake, stress resistance, and microbial activity.
   - Cost: $150/tree ($1,200 total).

### Site Logistics
- Materials delivered to the front of the property.
- Use of skid steer and manual tools for precise material placement.

### Material & Equipment Costs
- Compost: $357.00
- Mulch: $535.50
- Biochar: $400.00
- Tiger Sulfur: $50.00
- Fertilizer (14-7-7): $150.00
- MicroLife 6-2-4 Organic: $240.00
- Equipment Rentals & Logistics: $2,872.50

### Project Pricing
- Base Project Cost (8 Trees): $22,800
- Per Tree Cost: $2,850

### Optional Add-Ons

### Revenue Split – 65/35
```
