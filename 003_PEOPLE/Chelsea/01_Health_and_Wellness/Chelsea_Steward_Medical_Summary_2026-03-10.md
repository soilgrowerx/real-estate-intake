## Raw Data & Uncategorized Notes

### Dietary Protocol (Low Histamine)
- **Constraint:** Strict avoidance of high-histamine foods.
- **Triggers:** Fermented foods, aged cheeses, processed meats, citrus, spinach, tomatoes, avocados, and leftovers.
- **Freshness Primacy:** Meat must be frozen immediately or cooked fresh; leftovers are considered high-risk due to histamine buildup.

### The "Mechanical Trigger" Mechanism
- High concentrations of mast cells in the GI tract are sensitive to mechanical stress.
- Core/Abdominal workouts increase intra-abdominal pressure, causing localized mast cell degranulation.
- Leads to rapid release of histamine, prostaglandins, and leukotrienes (causing cramping/nausea).

### The Metabolic Blockade Logic
- **Cortisol Trap:** MCAS flares trigger cortisol, which directs fat to the midsection.
- **Cytokine Interference:** TNF-alpha and IL-6 release interferes with insulin signaling.
- **Fibrotic Adipose Tissue:** Adipose Tissue Mast Cells (ATMCs) can cause fat tissue to become fibrotic/stiff, resisting breakdown (lipolysis).
