**ASSISTANT**:
If a veteran is rated 100% permanent and total and passes away, the surviving family members may receive Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC) from the VA. The amounts vary based on the family&#x27;s composition and certain qualifying conditions:

1. **Surviving Spouse**: The basic monthly DIC rate for a surviving spouse is $1,612.75, effective December 1, 2023. This amount can increase with additional benefits such as:
   - $342.46 for an 8-year provision if the veteran had a totally disabling VA rating for 8 years prior to death and the marriage lasted these 8 years.
   - $399.54 for Aid and Attendance if the spouse has a disability requiring assistance with daily activities.
   - $187.17 for Housebound allowance if the spouse is unable to leave the house due to disability.
   - $342.00 for the first 2 years after the veteran&#x27;s death as a transitional benefit, and $399.54 for each eligible child under 18【50†source】【51†source】【52†source】【53†source】【54†source】【55†source】【56†source】.

2. **Surviving Children**:
   - Children between 18 and 23 in a qualified school program receive $338.49 monthly.
   - &quot;Helpless children&quot; over 18, who are permanently unable to support themselves due to a disability that occurred before age 18, receive $680.94 monthly.
   - The payment amount for children under 18 depends on the number of eligible surviving children. For example, one child receives $680.94 monthly, two children receive $489.79 each monthly, and so on, with the amount per child decreasing as the number of children increases【62†source】【63†source】【64†source】.

The DIC rates are effective as of December 1, 2023, and are subject to change annually. These benefits are tax-exempt, meaning no taxes are paid on these compensation payments.

**ASSISTANT**:
