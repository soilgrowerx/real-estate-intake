### Guiding Principles for Success

- Full-Time Mindset, Sustainable Pace: Aim for 6-9 credits (2-3 courses) per term. This is an ambitious but achievable full-time pace that will keep you moving quickly while balancing your work and family commitments.
    
- Plan Ahead, Stay Flexible: This roadmap is based on the course availability sheet provided. It is essential to always verify course offerings in the official OSU course catalog before each term's registration, as schedules can change.
    
- Communicate Proactively: Your success in undergrad was built on discipline. Apply that here. Schedule dedicated study blocks, communicate your schedule with your family, and maintain open lines of communication with your program coordinator and future advisor.
    
- The Critical Transition: The most important administrative step is moving from the certificate to the MNR program. As your welcome letter states, you must do this before your final term in the certificate to avoid having to reapply to OSU. This plan is built around that key milestone.

### Phase 2: The Transition to the MNR Degree

This is the most critical administrative phase. According to your documents, this should happen in the term before you take your final certificate class (SNR 570).

Key Goals:

1. Secure a faculty member to serve as your MNR Advisor.
    
2. Submit the "Change of Degree" form to officially add the MNR program.
    

Action Plan (To be completed during Spring/Summer 2026):

1. Contact Potential MNR Advisors: Based on your research, reach out to 2-3 faculty members. Send a professional email modeled on your previous application statements. Reintroduce yourself, state your interests (forest resilience, TEK, etc.), explain your goal to transition from the FCC certificate to the MNR, and ask if they would be willing to serve as your major professor.
    
2. Confirm Your Advisor: Once a faculty member agrees, notify the MNR Program Coordinator, Juliet Sutton.
    
3. Submit Change of Degree Form: Work with the coordinator to submit the necessary paperwork to add the MNR degree. This ensures you do not have to reapply to OSU. You will now be a dual-program student.
    

### Phase 3: MNR Core & Capstone Completion (Final 3-4 Terms)

Now you are officially an MNR student. The focus shifts to completing the remaining credits, which include the MNR core requirements and your capstone project.

Key Goals:

1. Complete the MNR core curriculum (18 credits).
    
2. Complete your FCC final project and MNR capstone project (9+ credits total).
    
3. Form your Graduate Advisory Committee (GAC).
    
4. Graduate!
